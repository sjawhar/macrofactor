# 01 — Setup, Arch, Leg Drive, Unrack (SBS Synthesis)

## Goal of setup

Create a stable platform that:

1. Reduces ROM enough to improve leverage.
2. Keeps shoulders in a safer, repeatable position.
3. Allows force transfer from feet/hips to bar.

## Step-by-step setup architecture

### 1) Equipment and rack settings

- **Bench friction first:** if shoulders slide, setup collapses. Add a grippy surface.
- **Hook height:** high enough to avoid deep shoulder reach, low enough to avoid mini pressout. Ideal: bar clears hooks by ~1–2 in.

### 2) Scapular position first

- Non-negotiable baseline: **retraction** (pinch shoulder blades together).
- Then tune elevation/depression for your shoulder comfort and pressing strength.
- Why this matters:
  - Chest sits higher (shorter ROM).
  - Bottom position is less provocative for many shoulders.
  - Better repeatability for touch point.

### 3) Arch creation

SBS describes two practical ways:

1. **Shoulders first, hips move back**
   - Set shoulders with bar roughly between forehead and mouth.
   - Brace on uprights.
   - Push hips back toward shoulders while lifting chest.

2. **Hips first, shoulders move down**
   - Start with bar nearer throat and shoulders slightly farther back.
   - Lock hips by foot pressure.
   - Push shoulders toward hips with chest high.

Key constraint: arch should come with **chest elevation**, not only lumbar extension.

### 4) Foot position options

SBS outlines three broad options:

1. **Feet in front** (least common)
   - Easier to keep butt down for some shorter lifters.
   - Often worse traction/drive.

2. **Feet far back (often on toes)**
   - Max arch potential.
   - Can reduce leg-drive quality in some lifters.
   - Heel-down federations may constrain this setup.

3. **Feet back and out (most common)**
   - Good compromise: arch + stability + drive.

### 5) Grip and wrist structure

- Default strength range often ~1.5–2x shoulder width.
- Place bar **low in hand** to reduce wrist extension.
- Crush-grip cue improves whole-body tension and bar control.

### 6) Bracing and breathing

- Deep diaphragmatic breath before descent.
- Keep torso rigid through rep to transmit leg drive.

### 7) Unrack execution

- With spotter: request controlled assist **up and out**, no yank.
- Without spotter: start slightly farther back on bench to avoid protracting/reaching.
- Settle bar over throat/upper chest start line before descent.

## Leg drive mechanics that actually matter

Leg drive is not random foot pushing. In SBS framing, it should:

- Help move chest **up and back into bar** at press initiation.
- Support the intended bar path (up-and-back first).
- Preserve butt contact and overall body position.

Useful cue cluster:

- “Heels through the floor”
- “Squeeze glutes”
- “Push chest back into the bar”

## Setup quality checklist

- [ ] Bench is grippy enough; no shoulder sliding.
- [ ] Hooks allow clean liftoff without shoulder protraction.
- [ ] Scapulae retracted and stable.
- [ ] Chest high with controlled arch.
- [ ] Feet placed for both stability and legal leg drive.
- [ ] Wrists stacked (not heavily cocked back).
- [ ] Bar settled over throat/upper chest before descent.

## Common setup mistakes and immediate fixes

| Mistake                                    | Why it hurts performance           | Fix                                  |
| ------------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| Slick bench                                | Position drifts every rep          | Add high-friction layer              |
| Hooks too high                             | Lose scap position on liftoff      | Lower hooks one slot                 |
| Overextended wrists                        | Pain + force leak                  | Bar lower in palm, wraps if needed   |
| Arching only lumbar                        | Less chest height, more discomfort | Drive sternum up while arching       |
| Feet too narrow/far forward for your build | Butt rise or no leg drive          | Move feet back-and-out progressively |
