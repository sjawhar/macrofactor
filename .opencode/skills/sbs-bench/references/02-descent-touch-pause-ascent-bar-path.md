# 02 — Descent, Touch, Pause, Ascent, and Bar Path (SBS Synthesis)

## Why this phase matters

Most bench outcomes (made/missed, comfortable/irritating) are decided by how you descend, where you touch, and your first inches off the chest.

## Descent

- Lower under control, typically around **2–3 seconds**.
- Maintain upper-back tension (“bend/rip the bar” cue can help some lifters).
- Controlled eccentric improves groove consistency and reduces unnecessary shoulder/pec tendon stress.

## Touch point

- Typical strong/safe zone: **nipple line to bottom sternum**.
- Many lifters perform best near lower sternum/just under pec line.
- Too high on chest often:
  - increases shoulder irritation risk,
  - worsens leverage,
  - and lengthens practical path in ways that hurt force transfer.

Consistency drill from SBS logic:

- Chalk center knurl and verify repeated touch mark location set-to-set.

## Pause styles

### Soft pause (default paused style)

- Touch lightly.
- Keep tension in pecs/delts/triceps.
- Slight chest-up intent preserved.
- Press without relaxing on chest.

### Sinking technique (advanced, niche)

- Controlled sink into chest while maintaining tension.
- Re-extend chest aggressively to launch bar.
- Can add bottom speed for skilled lifters but is harder to execute and has stricter comp-rule risk (heaving if sink continues after press command).

## Ascent mechanics

- Initiate aggressively with leg drive.
- Press **up and back** off chest.
- Transition to mostly vertical once bar is over upper chest/shoulders.

## SBS bar-path model

### Efficient pattern

1. Touch low chest.
2. Drive bar up-and-back toward upper chest/throat.
3. Finish vertically at lockout.

### Common novice error

1. Press straight up from chest.
2. Drift back late near midrange/lockout.
3. Hit unnecessary sticking through midrange.

This error increases shoulder-flexion burden when the lift is already hardest near the bottom.

## Elbows during this phase

- Descent: naturally more tucked.
- Early ascent: gradual flare to support up-and-back path.
- Mid/late ascent: elbows continue orienting out for better finish leverage.

Avoid extremes:

- Full-time flare + high touch = common shoulder aggravator.
- Heavy over-tuck (elbows far in front of bar) = inefficient and often stalls drive.

## Tempo and intent

- Eccentric: controlled.
- Concentric: maximal intent (“press hard/fast”).

SBS cites evidence that maximal concentric intent can meaningfully improve strength gains versus intentionally slower pressing with similar loading schemes.

## Quick fault map

| Symptom                  | Likely fault              | First correction                                   |
| ------------------------ | ------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------- |
| Miss 3–6 in off chest    | Straight-up first path    | Cue up-and-back + earlier elbow flare              |
| Shoulder pinch at bottom | High touch/flared descent | Lower touch, maintain retraction, slower eccentric |
| Inconsistent rep quality | Variable touch point      | Set explicit touch target and monitor marks        |
| Dead chest at pause      | Relaxing at sternum       | Keep soft pause tension and chest-up intent        |
