# 04 — Shoulder Safety, Pain, and Risk Management (SBS Synthesis)

## First triage (non-negotiable)

Stop self-management and refer out if any of the following are true:

- Pain is severe.
- Pain persists beyond ~2 weeks.
- Pain continues outside training.

This skill supports mild training irritation only.

## SBS protective framework

### Rule 1: Retract scapulae every rep

- Retraction creates a repeatable shoulder platform.
- Tune depression/elevation for comfort; retraction remains mandatory.

### Rule 2: Keep touch point low enough

- Typical zone: nipple to lower sternum.
- High touch with flared descent increases irritation risk for many.

### Rule 3: Control eccentric speed

- ~2–3s descents reduce chaotic loading and improve groove.

### Rule 4: Stay in your strongest pain-free pattern

- Natural tuck on descent, flare on ascent.
- Avoid rigid dogma that forces provocative joint angles.

## Frequent risk amplifiers

- Bench pad too slick (loss of shoulder/back position)
- Hooks too high (forced protraction/reach at unrack)
- Hyperextended wrists (distal stress transfer)
- Large sudden volume/intensity spikes
- Pressing dominance without matching pull volume
- Extreme bar touch positions to chase ROM shortcuts

## Mild irritation protocol (SBS-informed)

### Load management

- Reduce bench volume **30–50%** until symptoms calm.
- Keep intensity sub-threshold for painful ranges.

### Technical reset

- Rebuild setup sequence: retraction, chest-up arch, stable feet, controlled descent.
- Standardize touch in low chest zone.

### Supportive work

1. **Pulling volume:** at least 1:1 with pressing sets.
2. **External rotator training through internal-rotation ranges:** build usable ROM and control.
3. **Push-ups plus:** target serratus for scapular stability.
4. **Incline curls (stretch-focused):** improve biceps tissue tolerance through lengthened range.
5. **Light flyes (stretch-focused, high reps):** gentle pec mobility/strength at longer lengths.

SBS context for curls/flyes: conservative loading, controlled reps, brief stretch holds, typically higher reps (e.g., 15–20).

## Elbow pain often starts upstream

SBS notes medial elbow symptoms can reflect shoulder rotation limitations.

Signal:

- Pronated bench hurts elbow, but neutral/underhand pressing feels much better.

Response:

- Prioritize shoulder mechanics + tissue prep above, then rebuild loading.

## Tendon irritation guidance

For triceps/pec/biceps insertion irritation patterns:

- Short period away from heavy pressing.
- Gradual reloading.
- Controlled eccentric-focused work can help in tolerated ranges.

## Reverse-grip as a pain-management option

Can reduce symptoms for some lifters by changing shoulder/elbow orientation.

Cautions:

- Less secure grip under fatigue.
- More spotting/rack safety demands.
- Not legal in many federations.

## Return-to-normal progression

1. Symptoms down with reduced volume.
2. Rebuild full setup + path quality.
3. Reintroduce load first, then volume.
4. Keep pulling volume and movement variation in place to reduce recurrence risk.

## Coaching language that helps

Use:

- “Find your strongest pain-free path.”
- “Retract first, then press from a stable shoulder.”
- “Control down, explode up-and-back.”

Avoid:

- “Just force elbows in/out no matter what.”
- “Pain is normal; ignore it.”
