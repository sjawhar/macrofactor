# 06 — General Training Philosophy (SBS Lens)

## How SBS Interprets Evidence

SBS recommendations are typically **evidence-weighted, not evidence-binary**:

- Distinguish stronger evidence from weaker evidence.
- Separate population-average findings from what one lifter should do next week.
- Avoid absolutist claims when data are mixed.
- Favor robust defaults that work for most lifters under uncertainty.

## Rest Intervals

### Practical defaults

- Compound lifts: around **2-3 minutes** is a strong general default.
- Isolation/smaller movements: shorter rests are often fine.

### SBS nuance

- Hypertrophy data are mixed: shorter rests can work, but longer rests often preserve rep quality and productive volume.
- Useful decision rule: use the shortest rest that still keeps performance high enough for the goal.

## Exercise Selection (Machines vs Free Weights)

- Neither category is universally superior for hypertrophy or strength across all contexts.
- Free weights: often better for skill/coordination demands and sport specificity.
- Machines: often better for stable loading, local fatigue targeting, and hypertrophy volume with lower technical cost.
- SBS-consistent recommendation: use both as tools based on goal, constraints, injury history, and recovery.

## Periodization Basics

### High-level hierarchy

- **Linear progression**: effective early or in constrained contexts.
- **Undulating structures**: often outperform rigid linear models once novice progress slows.
- **Block periodization**: increasingly useful as lifters get advanced and need tighter fatigue/specificity management.

### SBS framing

- Periodization should solve practical problems (fatigue, specificity, peaking), not satisfy a theoretical template.
- The best plan is the one you can execute consistently while progressing priority lifts.

## Concurrent Training (Cardio + Lifting)

- Interference is real but usually **modest**, not catastrophic.
- Good programming matters more than cardio fear:
  - Manage total fatigue and weekly stress.
  - Separate hard endurance sessions from hard lifting when possible.
  - Match cardio mode and dose to goals.
- A better aerobic base often improves work capacity and between-set/session recovery.

## Accommodating Resistance (Bands/Chains)

- Bands/chains can shift loading toward stronger ROM positions and may help with specific strength qualities.
- For hypertrophy and broad strength outcomes, current evidence is largely **null/neutral** versus conventional loading when
  overall effort and volume are matched.
- Practical use: treat as a variation tool, not a magic upgrade.

## Programming Philosophy (Answer Style)

When questions are broad programming questions (not bench-technique troubleshooting):

1. Lead with broad SBS principles and practical defaults.
2. Give caveats only where they change the decision.
3. Avoid turning general questions into bench-centric answers.
4. State uncertainty explicitly when evidence is mixed.
