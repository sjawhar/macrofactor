# Conventional Deadlift — Setup and Execution (SBS synthesis)

## 1) Stance and feet

- Start around **hip width** (vertical-jump stance heuristic works for many).
- Adjust narrower/wider by comfort, force production, and torso/abdomen room.
- Toe angle is individual:
  - Slightly more toe-out may help off-floor pop for some.
  - Slightly more forward toes may help lockout mechanics for some.

## 2) Bar-to-foot relationship

- Start with bar roughly over **shoelaces / just in front of midfoot**, often ~1–2 inches from shins.
- Exact distance may shift with body mass and load level; priority is balanced pressure over midfoot.

## 3) Grip width and grip type

- Grip as narrow as possible without forcing knees in or hard arm-thigh grinding.
- Grip options:
  - Mixed grip (popular in PL).
  - Hook grip (high security, high discomfort initially).
  - Straps (excellent for volume when grip is limiter).
- Mixed-grip specifics:
  - Set bar near base of fingers (not deep palm).
  - Don’t “curl/row” underhand arm; keep upper arm relaxed.

## 4) Breathing and bracing

- Use deep diaphragmatic breath (360° torso expansion).
- Valsalva through rep; refresh at top or with bar resting on floor.
- “Brace like you’re about to be punched.”

## 5) Setup styles that tend to work best

SBS highlights multiple viable styles; for most lifters, the highest-yield are:

1. **Hamstring tension first, then pull into position**
2. **Back tight first, then pull hips into position (“compress spring”)**

Why: these naturally build full-body tension and reduce early-yank errors.

## 6) Tension before pull (critical)

- Don’t violently jerk the first inch.
- Gradually increase force so the bar feels like it will move with a tiny additional effort.
- This prevents hips-shoot-up and back-collapse patterns.

## 7) Balance and bar path

- Keep pressure centered at **midfoot**.
- If pressure drifts heels/toes, lockout gets harder and spinal position degrades.
- Useful drills:
  - 1" break-off hold + reposition.
  - Paused deadlifts (off floor + below knee).

## 8) Lats and scapular position

- Role is mostly mechanical repositioning, not direct spinal extension.
- Cues:
  - “Shoulder blades in back pockets.”
  - “Pull bar to shins.”
  - “Elbows to wall behind you.”
- Benefit: slightly reduces hip/lumbar demands and more notably thoracic demands.

## 9) Shin angle

- Perfectly vertical shins are **not mandatory**.
- Slight forward knee travel can work if knees clear bar path early.
- Choose based on what gives strongest break-off while preserving balance.

## 10) Execution cues by phase

### Start (first 2–4 inches)

- Chest up.
- Drive floor away.

### Mid-pull

- Keep chest up.
- Shoulders back + hips forward intent.

### Lockout

- Finish with hips and knees extended, torso upright.
- Avoid hyperextension and knee re-bend.
- Think “glute squeeze to neutral,” not “lean back forever.”

## 11) Eccentric (lowering)

- Lower under control with extension maintained.
- Benefits discussed by SBS:
  - Better hypertrophy stimulus.
  - Better movement quality and safety.
  - Better prep for next rep.
  - Less equipment abuse.

## 12) Common conventional miss patterns

- **Off floor:** setup/tension or off-floor strength issue.
- **At/just below knee:** common sticking point; often normal max-limit region.
- **Near lockout:** either spinal re-extension issue (round-backed pattern) or end-range hip extension output issue.
