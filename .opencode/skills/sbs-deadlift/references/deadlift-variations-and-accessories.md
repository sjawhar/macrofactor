# Deadlift Variations and Accessories (SBS synthesis)

## Primary patterns

## Conventional deadlift

- Higher back demand, similar hip demand vs sumo.
- Excellent for posterior-chain and back robustness.

## Sumo deadlift

- Higher quad demand, generally less back demand vs conventional.
- Useful for lifters limited by back fatigue/tolerance.

## Partial-range and ROM modifiers

## Block/rack deadlift

- Elevates bar to shorten ROM.
- Best uses:
  - Overload exposure.
  - Lower-back fatigue management.
  - Position-specific lockout work.
- Keep form honest; avoid turning into quasi-squat from pins.

## Deficit deadlift

- Elevates lifter feet to increase ROM.
- Useful when off-floor strength and position tolerance are limiting.
- Use carefully when mobility or lumbar tolerance is limited.

## Deadlift to knee

- Trains lower half with reduced full-lift fatigue.
- Useful for building strength in common sticking zones.

## Posterior-chain builders

## Romanian deadlift (RDL)

- Top-down hinge with slight knee bend.
- Strong hamstring and hinge-pattern builder with controllable fatigue.
- Also valuable for mobility/control development when eccentrics are slow and paused.

## Stiff-leg deadlift

- Similar to RDL but from floor each rep.
- Typically demands more mobility and positional control.

## Good morning

- High hinge challenge, strong erector + hip extensor stimulus.
- Useful as support work for position strength.

## Back raises / hyperextensions / reverse hypers

- Build erector endurance/capacity with lower technical barrier.
- Helpful for back-limited deadlifters.

## Hip thrusts / pull-throughs / swings

- End-range hip extension and glute force expression.
- Particularly useful for lockout issues.

## Specificity and technical drills

## Paused deadlifts

- Pause off floor and/or around knee.
- High-value for balance, bar path, and positional control.

## Tempo deadlifts

- Slower concentric/eccentric to reinforce groove and tension.
- Useful during technical rebuilds.

## Band and chain deadlifts

- Accommodating resistance increases challenge toward lockout.
- Can improve explosiveness and lockout force production.

## Reverse-band deadlifts

- Assistance at floor, less assistance near lockout.
- Useful for overload handling and lockout transition confidence.

## Snatch-grip deadlift

- Wider grip increases ROM and positional demands.
- Useful broad accessory for both off-floor and lockout development.

## Trap bar deadlift

- Neutral grip, often easier to learn.
- Usually allows higher peak force/power/velocity at comparable relative loads.
- Great for non-powerlifters; powerlifters still need straight-bar specificity.

## Grip-targeted accessory methods

- Timed deadlift holds with standard bar diameter.
- One-hand barbell holds.
- Weighted hangs.
- Farmers carries.

Support-grip specificity usually beats generic crushing-grip tools for deadlift transfer.

## Variation selection matrix (quick)

| Goal                      | Priority variations/accessories                                        |
| ------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Better off-floor strength | Deficits, paused off-floor deadlifts, RDL/position work                |
| Midrange stability        | Paused knee-level work, sumo positional loading, quad/glute support    |
| Lockout improvement       | Hip thrusts, block/rack pulls, band/chain strategies                   |
| Back capacity             | Conventional exposure, back raises/hyperextensions, rows/good mornings |
| Technique rebuild         | Pauses, tempo pulls, progressive ROM strategy                          |
| Reduce deadlift fatigue   | Secondary hinge work vs frequent heavy full deadlifts                  |
