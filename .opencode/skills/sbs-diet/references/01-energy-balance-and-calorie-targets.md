# Energy Balance and Calorie Targets (SBS Reference)

## Core Principle

Body composition change is driven by net body energy change, but day-to-day body weight is noisy due to water, glycogen, sodium, and GI-content shifts. Treat calorie targets as **iterative estimates**, not fixed truths.

## Energy Balance Components (TDEE)

- Basal metabolic rate (largest component; often ~70% in general population)
- Thermic effect of feeding (~10%)
- Exercise activity thermogenesis (~5%, highly variable)
- Non-exercise activity thermogenesis (~15%, highly variable)

These percentages are rough population-level anchors, not person-specific constants.

## Three Ways to Set Initial Intake

1. **Assume** (fast heuristic)
2. **Estimate** (equations + activity factors)
3. **Observe** (intake + body-weight trend over time)

Best practice: start with assume/estimate, then transition to observe.

## Assume Method (kcal per bodyweight)

| Goal pace       | kcal/lb/day | kcal/kg/day |
| --------------- | ----------: | ----------: |
| Aggressive cut  |          11 |        24.2 |
| Moderate cut    |          13 |        28.6 |
| Maintain        |          15 |        33.0 |
| Moderate gain   |          17 |        37.4 |
| Aggressive gain |          19 |        41.8 |

Maintenance usually lands around 14-16 kcal/lb for many people; 15 is the central default.

## Estimate Method (TDEE multipliers)

### For gaining

- Conservative/leaner gain: +5-10% above TDEE
- Typical gain: +10-20% above TDEE
- Aggressive gain: +15-20% above TDEE

Flat alternatives:

- Slow: +125 kcal/day
- Moderate: +250 to +375 kcal/day
- Fast: up to +500 kcal/day

### For losing

- Conservative: -10 to -20% below TDEE
- Aggressive: -30 to -40% below TDEE

Flat alternatives:

- Conservative: -250 to -500 kcal/day
- Moderate: -500 to -750 kcal/day
- Aggressive: -750 to -1000 kcal/day

Percentage methods scale better across very low/high expenditures.

## Observe Method (Target Rates)

- **Fat loss:** 0.25-1.0% bodyweight/week (aggressive >1.0%/week)
- **Weight gain:** 0.1-0.25% bodyweight/week (aggressive >0.25%/week)

Interpretation depends on context:

- Beginners with higher body fat can recomp while losing scale weight.
- Advanced lean lifters usually cannot gain quickly without notable fat gain.

## Why Calorie Targets Must Change Over Time

As body mass and tissue composition shift, expenditure shifts.

- During fat loss: expenditure often declines (less mass to carry, adaptive changes, lower TEF).
- During gain: expenditure can rise in some people (NEAT and other adaptive responses).

Therefore a fixed calorie target eventually drifts out of alignment.

## Practical Monitoring Standard

- Daily morning weigh-in (post-restroom, same scale, similar clothing)
- Daily intake logging
- Trend-based decisions (at least ~1 week of coherent data; often 1-2 weeks for confidence)
- Avoid reacting to isolated spikes/drops from sodium, carb, hydration, or GI changes

## Goal-Aligned Defaults

- If uncertain and risk-averse: start moderate (13 kcal/lb for cuts, 17 kcal/lb for gains)
- If timeline pressure high and tradeoffs acceptable: start aggressive
- For recomp: use a narrower energy range and focus on training quality, protein sufficiency, and trend interpretation
