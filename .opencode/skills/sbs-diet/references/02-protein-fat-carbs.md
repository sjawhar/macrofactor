# Protein, Fat, and Carbs (SBS Reference)

## Macro Priority Order

1. Set total calories
2. Set protein
3. Set fat with a minimum floor
4. Assign remaining calories to carbs based on performance demands and preferences

---

## Protein

### Daily Targets

- Lifters (hypertrophy/retention): **1.6-2.2 g/kg/day** (total body mass)
- Non-lifters: **1.2-1.8 g/kg/day**

### When to use FFM-based scaling

If body fat is substantially below/above average, FFM scaling is often cleaner:

- **2.0-2.75 g/kg FFM/day**

Broad practical extremes for healthy adults in the article:

- ~**1.25 g/kg FFM/day** (low activity, weight stable, higher body fat)
- ~**3.1 g/kg FFM/day** (lean, highly active, sizable deficit)

### Distribution and quality

- Aim for **3-6 protein feedings/day**
- Per feeding: **>=0.3 g/kg protein**, and roughly **2-3 g leucine**
- Plant-heavy diets can support hypertrophy if protein quantity, EAA complementarity, and dose distribution are handled deliberately

---

## Fat

### Typical target ranges

- Usually **20-35% of total calories**
- In very low/high calorie contexts: **0.7-1.5 g/kg/day** can be practical

### Absolute lower floor (height-based SBS approach)

- If height <150 cm: **30 g/day floor**
- If height >=150 cm:

```text
fat_floor_g_per_day = ((height_cm - 150) * 0.5) + 30
```

This floor is a minimum safety threshold, not the default intake target.

### Fat quality guidance

- Minimize artificial trans fats
- Keep a mix of saturated, monounsaturated, polyunsaturated fats
- Keep saturated fat generally around/under ~10% total energy in cardiometabolic-risk-sensitive contexts
- Ensure essential fatty acid coverage; EPA+DHA intake in practice often lands between guideline-level intakes and supplement-level intakes

---

## Carbohydrate

### Performance-driven carb targets

- Typical lifter: at least **3-4 g/kg/day** (if calories allow)
- Endurance-heavy training: at least **6 g/kg/day**

Why: high-intensity and/or high-volume training increases carbohydrate demand and glycogen turnover.

### Preference-driven carb targets

- Balanced default for non-performance-priority diets: **40-60% of calories**
- Low-carb: often **<=30% calories** (commonly <=200 g/day)
- Keto: usually **<=50-60 g/day**

Low-carb and keto are viable adherence options but not inherently superior for fat loss or hypertrophy at matched calories and protein.

---

## Alcohol and Macro Accounting

- Ethanol provides ~**7 kcal/g**
- A U.S. standard drink has ~**14 g ethanol** (~98 kcal from ethanol before carb additions)
- If incorporating alcohol in fitness-focused diets, replacing carbohydrate calories is often the least disruptive macro trade

---

## Macro Adjustment Rules During Dieting

### In fat-loss phases

- Keep protein stable/high
- Reduce fat and carbs together until fat floor is reached
- If more reduction needed after floor reached, reduce mostly carbs

### In gain phases

- Keep protein adequate and stable
- Raise carbs/fats based on preference, exercise demands, and appetite constraints
- If appetite is limiting, add energy-dense/liquid options strategically
