# Diet Patterns and Meal Timing (SBS Reference)

## Key SBS Position

Most named diet patterns are tools for improving adherence and food-structure fit. Their superiority claims usually fade when protein and calories are matched.

## Time-Restricted Feeding (TRF)

- TRF compresses intake into a daily window (often 4-8 hours).
- It can reduce calorie intake indirectly for some people.
- It is not reliably superior for fat loss under calorie-matched comparisons.
- Tradeoff: narrower window can reduce opportunities for evenly distributed protein feedings.

### If using TRF with physique goals

- Prefer an ~8-hour window when feasible
- Ensure ~3 meaningful protein doses inside the window
- Preserve total daily protein targets

## Intermittent Fasting (IF)

Common protocols include:

- Alternate-day fasting
- Periodic 2-day fasting
- Two nonconsecutive low/fast days weekly

Evidence trend in the article:

- Generally similar weight-loss outcomes to continuous restriction when average calorie deficit is similar.
- Not clearly superior; not clearly worse.
- Long fasting windows may be less favorable for lean mass retention if protein distribution becomes poor.

## Low-Carb and Keto

- Low-carb and keto can be effective for loss/maintenance if users adhere well.
- Not intrinsically superior for fat loss, hypertrophy, or health outcomes when calories and protein are matched.
- Potential practical downside: reduced high-intensity performance in carb-demanding sports/training.

Numeric anchors:

- Low-carb often <=30% of calories (commonly <=200g/day)
- Keto commonly <=50-60g/day carbs

## Vegan and Vegetarian Patterns

- Viable for health/performance/body composition when well planned.
- Main challenge is logistics (protein quality/completeness and micronutrient sufficiency), not impossibility.

Practical focuses:

- Meet total protein target
- Distribute protein in multiple robust doses
- Ensure essential amino acid complementarity
- Watch micronutrients frequently underrepresented in plant-heavy diets

## Carnivore and Paleo

### Carnivore

- Can be tracked and manipulated for energy/macro goals.
- Extremely restrictive and generally low-carb by default.
- SBS framing does not endorse avoiding plant foods given broad evidence supporting many plant foods.

### Paleo

- Can be configured to be healthy and nutritionally adequate.
- Imposes exclusions that are not strictly necessary for a healthy diet.

## Processed Foods

- "Processed" is too broad to classify as automatically good/bad.
- Ultra-processed, hyperpalatable foods increase overconsumption risk and can crowd out fiber/micronutrients.
- Practical strategy: base diet on nutrient-dense minimally processed staples, include processed foods strategically for adherence.

## Meal Timing for Performance and Body Composition

SBS emphasis hierarchy:

1. Hit daily calories and macros first.
2. Distribute protein feedings adequately.
3. Then optimize timing details if desired.

Meal timing helps most when it improves:

- Protein dose distribution
- Training fuel availability (especially carbohydrate)
- Adherence and consistency

## Choosing a Pattern (Decision Shortcut)

Pick the pattern that best satisfies all four:

1. You can sustain it long enough.
2. You can hit calories/macros within it.
3. It supports your training performance needs.
4. It preserves nutrient adequacy.

If a pattern fails any one of these repeatedly, modify or replace it.
