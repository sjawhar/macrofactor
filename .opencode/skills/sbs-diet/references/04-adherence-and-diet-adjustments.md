# Adherence and Diet Adjustments (SBS Reference)

## What Actually Breaks Diets

Not usually wrong equations. More often:

- Inconsistent intake tracking
- Reacting to daily scale noise
- Unsustainable restriction
- Ignoring adaptive expenditure shifts

## Standardized Monitoring Protocol

### Daily

- Log intake
- Weigh in each morning after restroom, before food/drink, same scale conditions

### Weekly interpretation

- Use trend data, not isolated days
- Watch for sodium/carb/hydration/GI-content confounders

## Rate Targets (for adjustment decisions)

- Fat loss target: **0.25-1.0% bodyweight/week**
- Aggressive fat loss: **>1%/week**
- Gain target: **0.1-0.25% bodyweight/week**
- Aggressive gain: **>0.25%/week**

These are guidance zones, not hard physiological laws.

## Plateau Protocol

1. Confirm compliance first (intake accuracy and execution consistency).
2. Rule out temporary water-weight noise (salt/carb/hydration shifts).
3. Require a meaningful trend window before acting (typically ~1-2 weeks).
4. Adjust calories only if trend misses target pace.
5. Reassess with another trend window.

## Adjustment Order by Goal

### Cutting

- Keep protein stable/high.
- Reduce calories mainly via fat + carbs.
- Continue reducing fat/carbs until fat floor reached.
- If more reduction required, further cuts mostly from carbs.

### Bulking

- Keep protein stable.
- Increase calories via fat and/or carbs according to training demands and preferences.
- If appetite is limiting, increase energy density (liquids, more palatable foods).

## Metabolic Adaptation Perspective

Energy needs are dynamic in all phases:

- In deficits: expenditure often declines over time.
- In surpluses: some people experience large compensatory increases in expenditure and appetite changes.

Hence, static plans fail eventually; iteration is required.

## Recomp-Specific Notes

Recomp feasibility is highest when:

- Training status is newer
- Body-fat stores are higher
- Protein and training quality are strong

Advanced lean lifters should expect slower/less dramatic simultaneous changes.

## Adherence Levers (Use before extreme macro edits)

- Simplify food environment
- Improve meal structure and predictability
- Shift food choices for satiety (cuts) or ease-of-intake (bulks)
- Use preferred diet pattern (TRF, low-carb, etc.) if it improves consistency without breaking macro goals

## When to Tighten Precision

High precision tracking is most useful when:

- Goal is aggressive and timeline-constrained
- Athlete is already lean
- Weekly rate targets are narrow
- Supplement calories and small tracking errors become non-trivial

## Decision Checklist Before Any Adjustment

1. Is adherence actually high?
2. Is trend window long enough?
3. Are confounders accounted for?
4. Is current rate outside target zone?
5. Can this be solved with a small calorie change instead of major overhaul?

If all five are yes, adjust.
