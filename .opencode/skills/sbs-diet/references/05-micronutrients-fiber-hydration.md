# Micronutrients, Fiber, and Hydration (SBS Reference)

## Micronutrient Framework

Use Dietary Reference Intake concepts pragmatically:

- Aim for **RDA** (or **AI** if RDA unavailable)
- Stay below **UL**

For most healthy people with varied diets and moderate training, this covers the basics.

## Higher-Risk Contexts for Micronutrient Gaps

Be extra attentive when:

- Calories are low
- Training volume is high
- Diet is vegan or heavily plant-based

Nutrients highlighted in the article for extra attention:

- Iron
- Calcium
- Magnesium
- Zinc
- Vitamin B12
- (for vegan-heavy diets also monitor vitamin D and iodine)

## Plant-Based Nuance

Plant-based diets are viable but may need deliberate planning for:

- Protein quality/complementarity
- Micronutrient density and absorption

Anti-nutrient context:

- Not a reason to avoid healthful plant foods.
- They can reduce absorption of some nutrients in some contexts.
- Food variety and preparation methods mitigate many issues.

## Fiber Targets and Individualization

Starting targets:

- **14 g fiber per 1000 kcal**, or
- Roughly **28 g/day (women)** and **36 g/day (men)**

Practical tolerance zone commonly observed: about **20-50 g/day**.

Adjustment logic:

- Low fiber + hunger/poor bowel regularity: increase gradually.
- High fiber + bloating/gas/discomfort: reduce or redistribute.

Fiber is partly fermentable; effective absorbed energy from carbs varies with fiber subtype. For practical coaching, keep both carb and fiber targets reasonably consistent day to day.

## Hydration Targets

General baseline:

- About **3-4 L/day** total water intake for many adults.

Reference intakes:

- Women: ~**2.7 L/day**
- Men: ~**3.7 L/day**

Needs increase with heat, training load, body size, and other factors.

## Practical Hydration Checks

- Urine should generally be pale/light and not strongly odorous.
- Large body-mass drops during sessions may indicate insufficient fluid replacement.

## Electrolytes

Most people do not need elaborate electrolyte manipulation if diet and hydration are reasonable.

More explicit management may be needed in:

- Extreme sweat loss
- Extreme environmental heat
- Very restrictive or unusual dietary patterns

## Weight-Tracking Interaction

Hydration and sodium/carb fluctuations can shift scale weight by pounds without true tissue change.

To reduce false conclusions:

- Standardize weigh-ins
- Track trend, not single days
- Note unusual sodium/carb/hydration events before modifying calories
