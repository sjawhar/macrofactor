# Health Markers and Hormones (SBS Reference)

## SBS Framing

Do not over-medicalize normal physiology.

- Acute marker fluctuations from hard training or dieting are often expected.
- The useful coaching question is usually: "What should we do this week?" not "How do we micromanage every biomarker?"
- Escalate to medical professionals when symptoms are clinically meaningful, persistent, or safety-relevant.

## Cortisol

### What is normal

- Training acutely raises cortisol; this is a normal stress-response feature, not automatically harmful.
- Temporary elevations around hard training blocks, poor sleep, or energy deficits can occur.

### When it becomes a concern

- Persistent high stress load with declining performance, poor recovery, sleep disruption, mood disturbance, and appetite changes can indicate under-recovery/overreaching.

### Practical SBS action

- Prioritize sleep duration/quality, recovery planning, and sensible training volume/intensity management.
- Maintain realistic calorie deficits (avoid needlessly extreme cuts for long periods).
- **SBS stance:** behavior-level levers beat "cortisol hacking" protocols for most users.

## Testosterone

### Baseline reality

- Normal variation is large across individuals and across time within the same individual.
- Single-point interpretation is noisy without context.

### Training and diet context

- Training can support endocrine health modestly, but do not expect dramatic natural hormone engineering from minor protocol tweaks.
- Aggressive prolonged deficits can depress sex hormones transiently.

### TRT context (coaching implication)

- Exogenous testosterone can change recovery profile and increase tolerance for higher training volumes in many cases.
- Programming still needs progression management; enhanced recovery is not unlimited recovery.
- **SBS stance:** account for context without pretending every outcome is explained by testosterone.

## Hypothalamic Amenorrhea / RED-S Context

### Core concept

- Hypothalamic amenorrhea often reflects low energy availability and systemic stress load (within RED-S context).

### Common setup

- Too-aggressive cuts, chronically low intake, high training stress, and insufficient recovery.

### Practical action

- Increase energy availability, reduce excessive training stress where needed, and prioritize recovery.
- **Medical referral is appropriate** for diagnosis and management; coaching alone is not enough.
- **SBS stance:** treat this as a health issue first, not a "diet compliance" issue.

## Contest Prep Bloodwork and Aggressive Cutting

### Typical pattern

- During deep contest prep/aggressive cuts, multiple hormones and health markers often worsen (sometimes substantially).

### Practical interpretation

- This is frequently a known consequence of prolonged low energy availability and very low body fat.
- Many markers improve after recovery/refeeding and return to less extreme body-composition targets.

### SBS stance

- Don't pretend severe prep is physiologically neutral.
- Use risk-aware planning, realistic timelines, and post-diet recovery phases.

## Bone Density

### Key drivers

- Resistance training is generally positive for bone health.
- Impact-loading activities can provide additional osteogenic stimulus.
- Adequate calcium (and overall nutrient sufficiency) matters.

### Population emphasis

- Bone-health attention is especially important for females over 40 and anyone with prolonged low energy availability history.

### SBS stance

- Lift, eat enough to support health/performance, and avoid chronic under-fueling.

## Decision Shortcuts for Responses

1. If question is about normal acute marker shifts after training, normalize physiology first.
2. If question is about chronic symptoms, prioritize behavior-level recovery changes and suggest medical follow-up when warranted.
3. If question is amenorrhea/RED-S/bloodwork pathology, avoid pseudo-medical certainty and recommend clinician involvement.
4. If question is TRT-specific, acknowledge altered recovery context and adjust programming expectations without hype.
