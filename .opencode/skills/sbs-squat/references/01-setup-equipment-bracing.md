# 01 — Setup, Equipment, and Bracing

## Setup Priorities

1. **Stable bar shelf** before anything else.
2. **Efficient walkout** (minimal steps).
3. **Predictable stance/toe setup**.
4. **Repeatable brace sequence**.
5. **Tripod foot and full-body tension**.

## Bar Shelf Details

### High bar

- Bar on traps; avoid direct pressure on C7.
- If neck discomfort: move bar slightly lower on trap mass or increase trap tension.

### Low bar

- Bar on posterior delts.
- If shoulder blade discomfort: adjust slightly above/below scapular spine.

### Front squat

- Bar on front-delt/clavicle shelf.
- **Protract** scapulae to move clavicles/shelf forward.
- Keep elbows high to prevent bar roll.

## Grip/Wrist/Elbow Notes

- Narrow grip improves upper-back tightness if pain-free.
- Wrist pain in back squat often = mobility + poor shelf issue.
- Common fixes: wider grip, thumbless grip, neutral wrist focus.
- Back squat elbow cue: elbows down/in for lat tension (or slightly up in low bar if shelf unstable).
- Front squat: elbows as high as possible, shoulders supporting load.

## Walkout Standards

- Rack height: neither calf-raise unrack nor half-squat unrack.
- Unrack aggressively with brace already set.
- Use 2-step or 3-step walkout; no wandering.

## Bracing Protocol (Rep-by-Rep)

1. Inhale diaphragmatically (abdomen/obliques expand).
2. Lock air with Valsalva.
3. Beltless: brace like incoming punch.
4. Belted: push 360° into belt.
5. Maintain spinal extension under load.

## Full-Body Tension Cues

- Foot tripod: big toe + pinky toe + heel.
- Hip torque cue options:
  - “Screw feet into floor”
  - “Spread floor apart”
- Upper back: “Bend the bar.”

## Equipment Guidance (SBS Practical)

- **Belt:** useful; usually improves performance by helping pressure generation.
- **Shoes:** solid sole mandatory. Heeled vs flat = individual preference + goal.
- **Knee wraps:** can add significant carryover with practice.
- **Sleeves:** mostly comfort/warmth/proprioception for many lifters.
- **Wrist wraps:** useful if wrist pain persists after technical fixes.
- **SSB/bands/chains:** useful tools, not prerequisites.
