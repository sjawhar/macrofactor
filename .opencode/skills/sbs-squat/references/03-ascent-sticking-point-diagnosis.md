# 03 — Ascent, Sticking Point, and Weak-Link Diagnosis

## Ascent Objective

Reach sticking point in a mechanically strong position, then grind through efficiently.

## Initiation Cues (Out of Hole)

- “Drive traps back into bar.”
- “Push feet through floor.”
- Keep torso angle controlled; avoid immediate hip rise.

## Sticking Point Reality

- Usually appears ~1-6 inches above parallel.
- Most misses happen here, not at true bottom.

## High-Value Sticking-Point Cue Pair

1. **Shoulders/traps back**
2. **Hips under (forward under) bar**

This keeps load from drifting fully into a good-morning finish.

## Panic Pattern to Avoid

- Hips keep rising while bar speed stalls.
- Result: lifter turns rep into inefficient good morning.

## Weak-Link Diagnosis

### Quad-limited signature

- Hips shoot up, knees drift back, torso increases lean from hole to sticking point.
- Interpretation: body reducing knee moment and shifting demand to hips.

### Bracing/core-limited signature

- Large squat:deadlift gap (>15-20%) with recurrent torso collapse pattern.
- Interpretation: bracing quality and/or thoracic extension strength limiting.

### Hip extensor-limited signature

- If quad and bracing factors are adequate, limit likely shifts to hips.

## Corrective Emphasis by Weak Link

- **Quads:** front squat, leg press/hack squat, unilateral knee-dominant work.
- **Core/bracing:** bracing drills + thoracic erector strengthening + technical rep practice.
- **Hips:** more squats plus good mornings/RDL/hip thrust/GHR/back extension.

## Effort Intent

Every rep should be lifted with maximal concentric intent while preserving position.
