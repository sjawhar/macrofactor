# Frequency, Splits, and Weekly Structure

## SBS frequency anchors

From the source article:

- Beginners learn lifts best with **2-4 exposures/week**
- Intermediates generally train muscles/movements **2-3x/week**
- Advanced lifters manipulate frequency across phases (higher in offseason, lower when peaking)

Frequency is primarily a tool for:

1. Better skill practice
2. Better volume distribution
3. Better fatigue management

## Minimum effective frequency by goal

| Goal                                  |                                  Typical frequency |
| ------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------: |
| Technique acquisition (beginners)     |                         3-4x/week per main pattern |
| Strength + hypertrophy (intermediate) |                       2-3x/week per pattern/muscle |
| Hypertrophy maintenance during peak   |                               1-2x/week per muscle |
| Specificity peaking (main lifts)      | 2-3x/week per competition lift, lower total volume |

## Split selection framework

Choose based on weekly time, recovery, and volume targets.

### 3-day full body

- Best for beginners and busy intermediates
- High frequency per pattern
- Moderate per-session fatigue

### 4-day upper/lower

- Best general intermediate template
- Easy to hit 2x frequency and 10-18 weekly sets
- Good fatigue balance

### 5-6 day PPL / body-part hybrids

- Useful when volume needs are high and recovery is strong
- Better for advanced intermediates who need more distribution

## Weekly structure patterns

### Beginner (3 days)

- Mon: squat + bench + hinge accessory
- Wed: squat variant + press + pull
- Fri: squat + bench variant + deadlift

Each main pattern appears 2-3+ times/week at manageable intensity.

### Intermediate (4 days UL)

- Mon Upper (strength bias)
- Tue Lower (strength bias)
- Thu Upper (hypertrophy bias)
- Fri Lower (hypertrophy bias)

Main lifts: heavy-moderate 75-85% exposures. Accessories supply most volume.

### Advanced (phasic)

- Offseason: higher frequency/variation (build specific work capacity)
- Pre-meet: lower volume/frequency, higher intensity, more specificity

## Per-session set caps (practical)

To preserve quality, keep hard sets per muscle per session roughly:

- Large muscles: 4-8
- Smaller muscles: 3-6

If you need more weekly sets, add frequency before massively increasing per-session sets.

## Frequency adjustment rules

- If repeated soreness/performance dips after high single-day volume: split work into more days
- If scheduling constraints force fewer days: lower per-muscle weekly volume to recoverable levels
- If technique degrades due to fatigue: increase exposure frequency with smaller sessions

## Common split mistakes

- One huge "bro day" per muscle with poor quality late sets
- Frequency too low for motor learning on main lifts
- Forcing advanced split complexity before recovery capacity is ready
