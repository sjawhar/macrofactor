# Intensity, Reps, and Exercise Selection

## Intensity and rep framework (SBS-aligned)

| Priority             |   Reps |   %1RM | RIR | Use case                               |
| -------------------- | -----: | -----: | --: | -------------------------------------- |
| Beginner technique   |    3-6 | 60-80% | 3-4 | Groove movement without breakdown      |
| Strength specific    |    1-6 | 75-90% | 1-3 | Main lifts and close variants          |
| Strength-hypertrophy |    5-8 | 70-82% | 1-3 | Compounds in intermediate blocks       |
| Hypertrophy base     |   6-15 | 60-80% | 0-3 | Accessory volume and tissue growth     |
| Endurance/low load   | 12-25+ | 30-65% | 0-3 | Local capacity and low-joint-cost work |

From the source article:

- Beginners: keep loads controlled, generally 60-80%, and avoid failure.
- Intermediate: main lift work mostly 75-85%; limit very light and very heavy work outside specific purposes.

## RIR standards by exercise type

- Competition/primary compound lifts: usually 1-3 RIR in training
- Secondary compounds: 1-3 RIR
- Isolations/accessories: 0-3 RIR depending on fatigue status

Repeated technical failure on compounds usually indicates load/fatigue mismatch, not effort deficiency.

## Exercise selection hierarchy

1. **Primary lifts** (goal specificity)
2. **Close variants** (address weak phase, improve skill)
3. **Accessory compounds** (high stimulus per fatigue)
4. **Isolation work** (target lagging muscles and improve muscular awareness)

## Compound vs accessory role split

### Compounds

- Drive specific strength expression and broad adaptation
- Highest priority for technical consistency
- Better with lower-moderate set counts at higher average intensity

### Accessories

- Carry much of weekly hypertrophy volume
- Reduce overuse stress from repeating competition patterns excessively
- Improve weak links and muscle balance

## Example lift menu by pattern

### Squat pattern

- Primary: competition squat
- Variants: pause squat, front squat, tempo squat
- Accessories: leg press, split squat, leg extension

### Press pattern

- Primary: bench press
- Variants: close-grip bench, pause bench, pin bench
- Accessories: DB incline press, machine press, lateral raises, triceps extensions

### Hinge/pull pattern

- Primary: deadlift
- Variants: deficit deadlift, RDL, opposite-stance deadlift
- Accessories: rows, pull-downs, ham curls, back extensions

## Selection criteria checklist

- Specificity: does it transfer to the goal?
- Progressability: can load/reps improve consistently?
- Tolerance: can joints/connective tissue handle it?
- Fatigue cost: does it fit this phase?
- Logistics: can it be executed consistently in your setup?

## Common errors

- Treating every set as max effort
- Overusing only competition lifts for all volume
- Ignoring individual structure and comfort in exercise choice
- Picking movements that cannot be progressed objectively
