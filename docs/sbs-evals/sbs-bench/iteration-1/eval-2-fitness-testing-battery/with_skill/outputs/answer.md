# The SBS Combine: A 12-Week Fitness Testing Battery

## Overview

An "SBS Combine" for a generally strong/fit/athletic person should measure **strength, power, work capacity, and movement quality** across the three primary barbell lifts and complementary modalities. Testing every ~12 weeks allows tracking of meaningful progress while accounting for training cycles and recovery. The battery below balances specificity (barbell strength), generality (metabolic capacity, mobility), and practicality (minimal equipment, repeatable).

---

## Core Strength Tests (Barbell)

### 1. **Bench Press 1RM**

- **Modality:** Flat barbell bench press, competition grip/pause style
- **Protocol:** Warm-up to 90%, then singles increasing by 5–10 lbs until a true max is reached (last successful rep with clean technique)
- **Why:** Direct measure of upper-body pressing strength; aligns with SBS bench framework emphasizing technical consistency and shoulder safety
- **Tracking:** Record weight, touch point consistency (chalk mark verification), and any technical breakdowns
- **Frequency:** Every 12 weeks

### 2. **Squat 1RM**

- **Modality:** Back squat (high-bar or low-bar, consistent stance)
- **Protocol:** Warm-up to 90%, then singles increasing by 5–10 lbs until max
- **Why:** Measures lower-body strength and core stability; foundational for athletic performance
- **Tracking:** Record weight, depth consistency, and knee/hip alignment quality
- **Frequency:** Every 12 weeks

### 3. **Deadlift 1RM**

- **Modality:** Conventional or sumo deadlift (choose one, stay consistent)
- **Protocol:** Warm-up to 90%, then singles increasing by 5–10 lbs until max
- **Why:** Measures posterior-chain and grip strength; complements pressing/squatting
- **Tracking:** Record weight, bar path consistency, and lockout quality
- **Frequency:** Every 12 weeks

---

## Accessory Strength Tests

### 4. **Bench Press 5-Rep Max (5RM)**

- **Modality:** Flat barbell bench, same setup as 1RM test
- **Protocol:** Warm-up, then load to a weight you can control for exactly 5 reps with clean technique
- **Why:** Measures strength-endurance and technical consistency under fatigue; more forgiving than 1RM for detecting form breakdown
- **Tracking:** Record weight and rep quality (any stalling, path deviation, or shoulder position loss)
- **Frequency:** Every 12 weeks

### 5. **Weighted Pull-Up or Dip (5RM)**

- **Modality:** Weighted pull-up (pronated grip) or weighted dip (chest-focused)
- **Protocol:** Warm-up, then add weight until you can perform exactly 5 reps with full ROM and control
- **Why:** Measures pulling strength and shoulder health (critical for bench press longevity per SBS framework); dips measure lockout triceps strength
- **Tracking:** Record total weight (bodyweight + added load), ROM quality, and any shoulder discomfort
- **Frequency:** Every 12 weeks

---

## Power & Rate-of-Force Development

### 6. **Vertical Jump (Peak Height)**

- **Modality:** Countermovement vertical jump, measured with tape or app
- **Protocol:** 3–5 maximal attempts, record best height
- **Why:** Measures lower-body power and neuromuscular coordination; sensitive to fatigue and recovery status
- **Tracking:** Record peak height in inches/cm; note any asymmetry or landing quality issues
- **Frequency:** Every 12 weeks

### 7. **Broad Jump (Distance)**

- **Modality:** Standing broad jump, measured from takeoff line to heel landing
- **Protocol:** 3–5 maximal attempts, record best distance
- **Why:** Measures horizontal power and lower-body explosiveness; complements vertical jump
- **Tracking:** Record distance; note consistency across attempts (high variance suggests fatigue or technique instability)
- **Frequency:** Every 12 weeks

---

## Work Capacity & Metabolic Fitness

### 8. **5-Minute Row Erg (Distance)**

- **Modality:** Concept2 rowing machine or equivalent
- **Protocol:** 5 minutes of continuous rowing at sustainable-but-hard intensity; record total meters
- **Why:** Measures aerobic capacity, upper-body endurance, and work capacity; low injury risk
- **Tracking:** Record total distance and average split time (500m pace)
- **Frequency:** Every 12 weeks

### 9. **Barbell Complex (Reps in 10 Minutes)**

- **Modality:** Alternating rounds of 5 power cleans + 5 front squats + 5 push presses (light-to-moderate load, ~60% of 1RM squat)
- **Protocol:** Complete as many rounds as possible in 10 minutes, maintaining technical quality
- **Why:** Measures integrated strength-endurance, movement quality under fatigue, and metabolic capacity
- **Tracking:** Record total rounds completed; note any form breakdown (early indicator of fatigue or technique issues)
- **Frequency:** Every 12 weeks

---

## Movement Quality & Mobility

### 10. **Shoulder Mobility Screen (SBS-Aligned)**

- **Modality:** Prone shoulder flexion ROM (arms overhead, lying face-down on bench)
- **Protocol:** Measure maximum pain-free overhead reach height relative to bench surface
- **Why:** Directly assesses shoulder health and bench press safety per SBS framework; early warning for impingement risk
- **Tracking:** Record ROM in inches/cm; compare to baseline and note any asymmetry
- **Frequency:** Every 12 weeks

### 11. **Scapular Retraction Strength (Prone Row Hold)**

- **Modality:** Prone incline bench, arms at 90° shoulder abduction, holding light dumbbell (10–15 lbs)
- **Protocol:** Retract scapulae maximally and hold for 30 seconds; assess ability to maintain position without fatigue
- **Why:** Tests scapular stability and endurance, critical for bench press setup and shoulder longevity (SBS Rule 1: retract scapulae every rep)
- **Tracking:** Record hold duration and any shoulder position drift; note if fatigue appears early
- **Frequency:** Every 12 weeks

### 12. **Hip Mobility Screen (90/90 Stretch)**

- **Modality:** 90/90 hip stretch (one leg flexed 90° in front, one leg flexed 90° behind)
- **Protocol:** Hold each side for 30 seconds, assess depth and symmetry
- **Why:** Measures hip mobility and identifies asymmetries that may affect squat/deadlift mechanics
- **Tracking:** Record ROM quality and any pain/restriction; compare left vs right
- **Frequency:** Every 12 weeks

## Testing Protocol & Logistics

### Scheduling

- **Day 1:** Barbell maxes (bench 1RM, squat 1RM, deadlift 1RM) — allow 2–3 hours, full recovery between lifts
- **Day 2:** Accessory strength (bench 5RM, pull-up/dip 5RM) — 24 hours after Day 1
- **Day 3:** Power tests (vertical jump, broad jump) — 48 hours after Day 1, fresh state
- **Day 4:** Work capacity (5-min row, barbell complex) — 72 hours after Day 1
- **Day 5:** Mobility screens (shoulder, scapular, hip) — can be same day as work capacity or separate

### Conditions

- **Nutrition:** Eat normally; avoid testing in fasted state or immediately post-meal
- **Sleep:** Aim for 7–9 hours the night before testing
- **Warm-up:** 5–10 min light cardio, then movement-specific warm-up for each test
- **Environment:** Consistent location, temperature, and equipment (same bench, rack, rower, etc.)
- **Spotters:** Use for barbell maxes; have a training partner present for safety and consistency

---

## Tracking & Interpretation

### Spreadsheet Columns

- **Test name, date, result, notes (form quality, fatigue level, environmental factors)**
- **Trend line:** Plot results over 3–4 testing cycles to identify progress direction
- **Ratio checks:** Bench 1RM ÷ Squat 1RM (typically 0.6–0.75 for strong athletes); Deadlift 1RM ÷ Squat 1RM (typically 1.2–1.4)

### Red Flags

- **Strength plateau or decline** across multiple lifts → likely overtraining, underfeeding, or sleep deficit
- **Power tests declining while strength stable** → fatigue accumulation or CNS fatigue
- **Work capacity dropping** → metabolic stress or inadequate recovery
- **Mobility loss** → potential injury risk or postural compensation; consider deload or PT referral
- **Form breakdown under fatigue** → technical coaching needed or volume too high

---

## Why This Battery Works

1. **Specificity:** Barbell lifts measure what matters for strength athletes; SBS bench framework ensures shoulder safety is monitored
2. **Generality:** Power, work capacity, and mobility tests catch imbalances and overtraining signals
3. **Repeatability:** All tests are simple, require minimal equipment, and can be performed in any gym
4. **Sensitivity:** 12-week cycles are long enough to show meaningful progress but short enough to catch problems early
5. **Actionability:** Results directly inform programming adjustments (e.g., if bench stalls, diagnose via SBS framework; if mobility drops, add mobility work)

## Conclusion

The SBS Combine is a practical, evidence-based testing battery that measures the full spectrum of athletic fitness: absolute strength, power, work capacity, and movement quality. By testing every 12 weeks, you create a feedback loop that informs programming, detects overtraining early, and ensures long-term progress without injury. The emphasis on shoulder mobility and scapular stability aligns with SBS bench press safety principles, making this battery particularly valuable for lifters prioritizing both performance and longevity.
