# The SBS Combine: A Comprehensive Fitness Testing Battery

A "SBS Combine" for a generally strong, fit, and athletic person should assess the key pillars of athletic performance: maximal strength, power, speed, muscular endurance, aerobic capacity, and movement quality. Here's a practical testing battery designed for quarterly (12-week) reassessment:

## Strength Tests

**1. Back Squat 1RM (or estimated max)**

- Establishes lower body maximal strength baseline
- Test every 12 weeks; track absolute load and relative strength (load/bodyweight)
- Provides context for lower body power and endurance tests

**2. Bench Press 1RM (or estimated max)**

- Upper body pressing strength standard
- Same protocol as squat; compare to bodyweight for relative strength
- Correlates with upper body power output

**3. Deadlift 1RM (or estimated max)**

- Full-body posterior chain strength
- Often the highest absolute load; track carefully for form integrity
- Indicates hip extension power and spinal stability

## Power Tests

**4. Vertical Jump (countermovement jump)**

- Measure max height reached or distance from standing reach
- Excellent indicator of lower body power and rate of force development
- Highly repeatable; minimal equipment needed
- Target: 20-30 inches for athletic individuals; track +/- 1-2 inches as meaningful change

**5. Broad Jump (standing long jump)**

- Horizontal power expression; less affected by arm swing than vertical jump
- Measure distance from toe line to nearest heel contact
- Target: 8-10 feet for athletic individuals; track in 3-6 inch increments

**6. Medicine Ball Chest Pass (explosive upper body power)**

- Throw a 10-14 lb medicine ball for maximum distance from chest
- Measure distance or count reps in 30 seconds at moderate intensity
- Assesses upper body power and core stability

## Speed & Agility

**7. 40-Yard Dash (or 30-meter sprint)**

- Straight-line speed; foundational athletic quality
- Time from standing start; electronic timing preferred for accuracy
- Target: sub-5 seconds for athletic individuals; track to 0.1 second precision

**8. 5-10-5 Shuttle (or T-Drill)**

- Lateral agility and deceleration capacity
- Run 5 yards, back 10 yards, back 5 yards; time the full sequence
- Highly sport-relevant; reveals asymmetries and change-of-direction ability
- Target: 4.5-5.5 seconds for athletic individuals

## Muscular Endurance

**9. Max Reps Bench Press at 185 lbs (or 60% estimated 1RM)**

- Submaximal strength endurance; less joint stress than 1RM testing
- Perform as many reps as possible with good form
- Track total reps; improvements indicate better work capacity

**10. Max Reps Back Squat at 225 lbs (or 60% estimated 1RM)**

- Lower body muscular endurance
- Same protocol as bench; watch for form breakdown as fatigue accumulates

## Aerobic Capacity

**11. 2-Mile Run (or 3K run)**

- Gold standard for aerobic fitness in non-sport-specific populations
- Time to completion; track improvements in pace
- Target: sub-14 minutes for athletic individuals; 1-2 minute improvements over 12 weeks indicate real gains

**Alternative: 1000-meter row**

- Excellent full-body aerobic test; less impact than running
- Highly repeatable on rowing machine; track split times and total time

## Movement Quality (Optional but Valuable)

**12. Overhead Squat Assessment**

- Bodyweight squat with light load overhead (PVC pipe or empty bar)
- Assess mobility, stability, and movement pattern quality
- Not timed; qualitative assessment of ankle/hip/thoracic mobility and core control

## Testing Protocol Recommendations

- **Frequency**: Every 12 weeks (quarterly)
- **Spacing**: Conduct all tests within 3-5 consecutive days; allow 48 hours between heavy strength tests
- **Warm-up**: 5-10 minutes light cardio + dynamic stretching before testing
- **Recovery**: Ensure 48-72 hours of reduced training volume before test day
- **Consistency**: Same time of day, same equipment, same testing order each cycle
- **Documentation**: Record exact loads, distances, times, and environmental conditions (temperature, fatigue level, sleep quality)

## Expected Progress

Over 12 weeks of consistent training, expect:

- Strength gains: 5-10% improvement in 1RM lifts
- Power: 3-5% improvement in jump height/distance
- Speed: 0.1-0.3 second improvement in sprint times
- Endurance: 1-2 minute improvement in 2-mile run time

This battery provides comprehensive assessment of athletic qualities while remaining practical for self-administration. The combination of absolute and relative measures allows tracking of both raw performance and efficiency relative to bodyweight.
