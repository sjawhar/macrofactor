# Answer: Steps Taper + Calorie Adjustment

## The Core Issue

When you reduce activity (steps from 25k+ down), your **total daily energy expenditure (TDEE) declines**. If you keep calories the same, you're no longer in the same energy balance state—you've shifted toward a surplus relative to your new, lower expenditure.

This **will** affect your energy balance and body composition trajectory.

## Why This Matters

From the SBS framework:

> "As body mass and tissue composition shift, expenditure shifts. During fat loss: expenditure often declines (less mass to carry, adaptive changes, lower TEF). During gain: expenditure can rise in some people (NEAT and other adaptive responses). Therefore a fixed calorie target eventually drifts out of alignment."

**Non-exercise activity thermogenesis (NEAT)** is roughly 15% of TDEE in the general population and is highly variable. A 25k+ step reduction is a massive NEAT cut—potentially 300–500+ kcal/day depending on your body weight and pace.

## What Happens If You Don't Adjust

- **If you were in a deficit**: Keeping calories the same shrinks the deficit, slowing fat loss.
- **If you were at maintenance**: Keeping calories the same creates a surplus, causing weight gain.
- **If you were in a surplus**: Keeping calories the same increases the surplus, accelerating weight gain.

## What You Should Do

1. **Estimate the TDEE shift**: A rough rule is that 10,000 steps ≈ 300–500 kcal for most people (varies by body weight and pace). Dropping from 25k to, say, 10k steps is roughly a **450–750 kcal/day reduction in expenditure**.

2. **Adjust calories to match your goal**:
   - **If cutting**: Reduce calories by roughly 50–75% of the step reduction (e.g., if steps drop ~500 kcal, cut ~250–375 kcal from intake). This keeps you in a deficit but prevents the deficit from becoming too aggressive.
   - **If maintaining**: Reduce calories by roughly the full step reduction to stay at maintenance.
   - **If bulking**: Reduce calories by roughly the full step reduction to keep the surplus stable.

3. **Use the SBS Plateau Protocol**:
   - Log intake daily.
   - Weigh in each morning (post-restroom, same scale, same conditions).
   - Track your weight trend over **1–2 weeks** after the step reduction and calorie adjustment.
   - If your rate is slower/faster than target, adjust calories again (reduce fat + carbs together; keep protein stable).

## Macro Adjustment Order

When you reduce calories due to lower activity:

- **Keep protein stable/high** (1.6–2.2 g/kg/day if lifting; 1.2–1.8 g/kg/day if not).
- **Reduce fat and carbs together** until you hit your fat floor (height-based: `((height_cm - 150) × 0.5) + 30` grams/day minimum).
- **If more reduction is needed**, cut mostly from carbs.

## Key Takeaway

**No, you cannot keep calories the same without messing up your energy balance.** A 25k+ step reduction is a substantial TDEE cut. Adjust calories downward to match your new expenditure and your goal (cut/maintain/gain). Then monitor your weight trend and iterate if needed.
