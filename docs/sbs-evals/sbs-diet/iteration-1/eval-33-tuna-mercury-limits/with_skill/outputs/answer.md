# Mercury in Canned Tuna: Safe Weekly Intake

## The Bottom Line

**Light canned tuna is safe to eat 2–3 times per week (roughly 6–9 oz/week).** Albacore (white) tuna should be limited to **1 time per week (6 oz max)** due to higher mercury content. This aligns with FDA and EPA guidance and allows you to keep tuna as a cheap, convenient protein source without accumulating unsafe mercury levels.

---

## Why Mercury Matters

Mercury is a neurotoxin that accumulates in your body over time. Fish absorb it from water and sediment, and larger predatory fish (like tuna) accumulate higher concentrations because they eat smaller fish. Chronic exposure can damage the nervous system, kidneys, and developing brains in children and pregnant people.

The FDA and EPA set safe intake limits based on body weight and mercury concentration in different fish species.

---

## Tuna Types and Mercury Content

### Light Canned Tuna (Skipjack)

- **Mercury level:** ~0.12 ppm (parts per million)
- **Safe intake:** 2–3 servings per week (6 oz = 1 serving)
- **Why:** Lower mercury than albacore; safe for regular consumption

### Albacore (White) Canned Tuna

- **Mercury level:** ~0.32 ppm (roughly 3× higher than light tuna)
- **Safe intake:** 1 serving per week maximum (6 oz)
- **Why:** Higher mercury concentration requires stricter limits

### Fresh/Frozen Tuna Steaks

- **Mercury level:** ~0.38 ppm (highest of the three)
- **Safe intake:** 1 serving per week maximum (6 oz)
- **Why:** Larger fish, higher bioaccumulation

## How to Stay Safe

### Weekly Tuna Intake Plan

**Option 1: Light Tuna Focus (Cheapest & Safest)**

- Eat light canned tuna **2–3 times per week** (12–18 oz total)
- Occasional albacore (white) tuna **0–1 time per week** (0–6 oz)
- **Total weekly mercury:** Well below safe limits

**Option 2: Mixed Approach**

- Light tuna **2 times per week** (12 oz)
- Albacore tuna **1 time per week** (6 oz)
- **Total weekly mercury:** Safe, with more variety

**Option 3: Albacore Only**

- Albacore tuna **1 time per week** (6 oz max)
- Avoid other high-mercury fish that week
- **Total weekly mercury:** At the safe limit

### Practical Tips

1. **Rotate with other proteins:** Don't eat tuna every day. Mix in chicken, eggs, ground beef, or other fish (salmon, cod, tilapia are lower in mercury).

2. **Check the label:** Light tuna is cheaper and safer. Albacore (white) is pricier and higher in mercury—avoid it if budget is your main concern.

3. **Drain the liquid:** Canned tuna packed in water is fine; oil-packed is also safe but higher in calories. Draining reduces sodium slightly.

4. **Track weekly, not daily:** Mercury accumulates over weeks and months, not days. One serving won't hurt; consistency over time is what matters.

5. **Special populations:** If you're pregnant, nursing, or feeding young children, stick to light tuna and limit to 2 servings per week max. Developing brains are more sensitive to mercury.

## Why This Fits Your Situation

You mentioned tuna is **cheap and convenient**—light canned tuna checks both boxes:

- **Cost:** ~$0.50–$1.00 per can (excellent protein-per-dollar)
- **Convenience:** No prep, shelf-stable, ready to eat
- **Protein:** ~20 g per 5 oz can (fits easily into daily targets)
- **Safety:** 2–3 cans per week is well within safe limits

At 2–3 servings per week, you're getting **40–60 g of protein from tuna alone**, which is substantial for a cheap source, while staying well below mercury accumulation risk.

## The SBS Framework Applied

From the **Micronutrients, Fiber, and Hydration** reference:

> "For most healthy people with varied diets and moderate training, [hitting RDA/AI and staying below UL] covers the basics."

Mercury safety is a **UL (upper limit) issue**—you want to stay _below_ the safe threshold. By eating light tuna 2–3 times per week and rotating other proteins, you hit your protein targets while respecting the mercury ceiling.
