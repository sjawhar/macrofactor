# L-Citrulline: Dosing and Timing Guide

## Should You Take L-Citrulline?

L-citrulline is an amino acid that may be worth considering if you're interested in:

- **Improved blood flow and vasodilation** — citrulline is converted to nitric oxide, which relaxes blood vessels
- **Enhanced exercise performance** — particularly for endurance and high-rep work
- **Reduced fatigue** — may help with ammonia clearance during intense training
- **Erectile function** — some evidence suggests benefits for blood flow-dependent performance

It's generally considered safe for most people, but it's not essential for fitness or health. Your results will depend on training quality, nutrition, and recovery—citrulline is a supplement, not a replacement for fundamentals.

## Dosing

**Standard effective dose: 6–8 grams per day**

- **Lower range (3–5g)**: May provide modest benefits; some people respond well to lower doses
- **Higher range (8–10g)**: More commonly used in research; diminishing returns above 10g
- **Citrulline malate vs. L-citrulline**: Citrulline malate (6–8g) includes malate, which may aid ATP recovery; L-citrulline alone requires slightly higher doses (8–10g) for equivalent nitric oxide production

Most people use **6–8g of L-citrulline or 8g of citrulline malate** as a practical middle ground.

## Timing

**Pre-workout (30–60 minutes before training)** is most common:

- Allows time for absorption and conversion to nitric oxide
- Peaks in the bloodstream during your workout
- Best for maximizing blood flow during exercise

**Consistency matters more than perfect timing**:

- Taking it daily (even if not training) may provide cumulative benefits
- Some evidence suggests benefits for general cardiovascular health with daily use
- If you train inconsistently, daily dosing may be more practical than trying to time it pre-workout

**Post-workout** is less ideal but not ineffective—the primary benefit window is during exercise.

## Practical Recommendations

1. **Start with 6–8g** taken 30–60 minutes before training
2. **Mix with water or juice** — it's relatively tasteless but mixes better in liquid
3. **Take consistently** — benefits build over 1–2 weeks of regular use
4. **Expect modest gains** — improved pump, slightly better endurance, reduced fatigue; not a dramatic performance multiplier
5. **Monitor tolerance** — most people tolerate it well; some report mild GI effects at higher doses

## Bottom Line

L-citrulline is a reasonable supplement choice if you're looking for a modest edge in blood flow and exercise performance. It's safe, affordable, and has decent research support. Dosing of 6–8g pre-workout is the standard approach. However, it's not necessary for results—prioritize training consistency, progressive overload, and adequate nutrition first.
