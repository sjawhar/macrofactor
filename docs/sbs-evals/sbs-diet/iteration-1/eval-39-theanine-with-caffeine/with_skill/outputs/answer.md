# How Should I Use L-Theanine with Caffeine?

## SBS Diet Framework Response

The SBS Diet skill focuses on **energy balance, macronutrient targets, meal timing, adherence, and micronutrient/hydration adequacy**. L-theanine and caffeine are **not primary diet variables** in the SBS framework—they fall outside the core decision hierarchy of calories → protein → fat → carbs → health coverage.

However, here's how to think about them within an SBS context:

---

## What the SBS Framework Covers

The SBS Diet skill prioritizes:

1. **Calorie targets** (energy balance for body composition)
2. **Protein** (1.6–2.2 g/kg/day for lifters; 1.2–1.8 g/kg/day for non-lifters)
3. **Fat** (20–35% of calories, with a height-based minimum floor)
4. **Carbohydrates** (3–4 g/kg/day for lifters; 6+ g/kg/day for endurance athletes)
5. **Micronutrients, fiber, and hydration** (RDA targets, 14 g fiber per 1000 kcal, 3–4 L water/day)

---

## What L-Theanine and Caffeine Are Not

- **Not macronutrients** (they don't contribute meaningfully to calories, protein, fat, or carbs)
- **Not covered in the SBS framework** (the skill references do not address nootropics, stimulants, or ergogenic aids)
- **Not a diet-adjustment lever** (they don't replace adherence to calorie/macro targets or training quality)

---

## SBS Position on Supplements and Ergogenic Aids

The SBS framework emphasizes **foundational priorities first**:

1. **Hit your calorie and macro targets consistently** (this is where 80%+ of results come from)
2. **Ensure adequate protein distribution** (3–6 feedings/day, ≥0.3 g/kg per feeding)
3. **Cover micronutrient and hydration basics** (RDA, fiber, water)
4. **Train hard and recover well** (sleep, stress, consistency)

Only after these are locked in does the SBS framework consider optimizing details like meal timing, diet patterns, or supplementation.

---

## What You Should Do Instead

If you're asking about L-theanine + caffeine, you likely have one of these goals:

### Goal: Better focus or energy during training

- **SBS answer:** Ensure adequate carbohydrate intake (3–4 g/kg/day for lifters) and hydration (3–4 L/day). These are the primary fuels for performance.
- **Secondary:** Ensure adequate sleep and manage training volume to avoid overtraining.

### Goal: Fat loss with sustained energy

- **SBS answer:** Set a moderate calorie deficit (13 kcal/lb or 28.6 kcal/kg), keep protein high (1.8–2.2 g/kg/day), and maintain adequate carbs for training performance. Adherence to these targets matters far more than any supplement.

### Goal: Appetite suppression during a cut

- **SBS answer:** Prioritize satiety-promoting foods (protein, fiber, whole grains, vegetables). Adjust meal timing and structure for adherence. If appetite is still problematic, reduce calories gradually rather than relying on stimulants.

---

## Bottom Line

**L-theanine and caffeine are outside the scope of the SBS Diet framework.** The framework is built on energy balance, macronutrient targets, and health coverage—not on nootropics or stimulants.

If you want to use them, that's a separate question for a sports nutrition or pharmacology resource. But within the SBS framework, **focus on hitting your calorie, protein, carb, and hydration targets first.** That's where the real results come from.
