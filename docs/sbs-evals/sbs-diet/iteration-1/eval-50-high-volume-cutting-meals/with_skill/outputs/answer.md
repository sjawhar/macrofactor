# High-Volume, Low-Calorie Meals for Cutting and Getting Very Lean

## Core Principle: Satiety Through Volume, Not Restriction

The key to staying full while cutting is **food volume and satiety density**, not deprivation. High-volume, low-calorie foods allow you to eat larger portions while maintaining a calorie deficit—critical for adherence and hunger management during aggressive cuts.

---

## Why Volume Matters During Cuts

When you're in a calorie deficit (especially aggressive ones >1% bodyweight/week), hunger and fullness signals become more pronounced. Research and practical experience show that:

- **Portion size** and **meal volume** trigger satiety signals more reliably than calorie count alone
- **Fiber** and **water content** in foods expand stomach volume without adding calories
- **Protein** at each meal (≥0.3 g/kg per feeding) enhances satiety and preserves lean mass during deficit
- **Whole foods** (minimally processed) tend to be less energy-dense than ultra-processed alternatives

### 4. **Fats (Strategic, Minimal)**

During aggressive cuts, fat intake often drops. Maintain a **height-based minimum floor** to preserve hormone function and nutrient absorption.

**Fat floor calculation:**

- If height ≥150 cm: `fat_floor_g = ((height_cm - 150) × 0.5) + 30`
- Example: 180 cm tall → floor = ((180 − 150) × 0.5) + 30 = **45 g/day**

| Food                      | Calories | Fat    | Best Use                  |
| ------------------------- | -------- | ------ | ------------------------- |
| Olive oil (1 tsp)         | 40       | 4.5g   | Dressing, cooking         |
| Avocado (1/4)             | 60       | 5.5g   | Salads, toast             |
| Nuts (1 oz/28g)           | 160–200  | 14–20g | Snack (measure carefully) |
| Fatty fish (salmon, 100g) | 206      | 13g    | Omega-3 source, protein   |
| Whole eggs (1)            | 78       | 5.5g   | Breakfast, versatile      |
| Peanut butter (1 tbsp)    | 96       | 8g     | Snack (measure carefully) |

**Strategy:** Use cooking oils sparingly (1–2 tsp per meal). Get fats from protein sources (fish, eggs) when possible. Avoid adding extra fat during aggressive cuts unless you're below the floor.

## Satiety Strategies Beyond Food Choice

### 1. **Protein Distribution**

- Aim for **3–6 protein feedings per day**
- Each feeding: **≥0.3 g/kg protein** (e.g., 21g for a 70 kg person)
- Protein is the most satiating macronutrient; prioritize it at every meal

### 2. **Fiber Intake**

- Target **14 g per 1000 kcal** (roughly **28–36 g/day** for most people)
- Fiber expands in the stomach and slows gastric emptying, enhancing fullness
- Increase gradually to avoid GI discomfort

### 3. **Hydration**

- Drink **3–4 L water daily** (baseline; adjust for training and heat)
- Water-rich foods (vegetables, fruits) add volume without calories
- Adequate hydration supports satiety signals

### 4. **Meal Structure**

- Eat **whole foods** (minimally processed) as your base
- Avoid ultra-processed, hyperpalatable foods—they're engineered to override satiety
- Prepare meals at home when possible for better portion control

### 5. **Eating Speed and Mindfulness**

- Eat slowly; satiety signals take ~20 minutes to register
- Chew thoroughly; larger bites and longer meals enhance fullness perception
- Minimize distractions (screens) during meals

### 6. **Meal Timing (If Helpful)**

- Some people find **time-restricted feeding** (eating within an 8-hour window) helpful for adherence
- Others prefer **evenly distributed meals** throughout the day
- Choose what works for your schedule and hunger patterns—consistency matters more than timing

## Calorie and Macro Targets for Aggressive Cutting

### **Starting Point (SBS Assume Method)**

- **Aggressive cut:** 11 kcal/lb bodyweight/day (24.2 kcal/kg)
- **Moderate cut:** 13 kcal/lb bodyweight/day (28.6 kcal/kg)

Example for an 80 kg person:

- Aggressive: 1,936 kcal/day
- Moderate: 2,288 kcal/day

### **Macro Targets**

- **Protein:** 1.8–2.2 g/kg/day (higher end if very lean or in large deficit)
  - For 80 kg: 144–176g/day
- **Fat:** Maintain height-based floor; typically 20–35% of calories
  - For 80 kg at 180 cm: minimum 45g/day
- **Carbs:** Fill remaining calories; typically 3–4 g/kg/day if training hard
  - For 80 kg: 240–320g/day

### **Adjustment Protocol**

1. **Track daily intake** and **weigh in each morning** (post-restroom, same scale)
2. **Wait 1–2 weeks** of trend data before adjusting
3. **Target rate:** 0.25–1.0% bodyweight/week (>1% is aggressive with tradeoffs)
4. **If losing too slowly:** Reduce calories via **carbs + fats together** (keep protein stable)
5. **If losing too fast:** Increase calories slightly via carbs (especially if training performance drops)

## Foods to Minimize During Aggressive Cuts

- **Oils and added fats** (beyond cooking needs and fat floor)
- **Nuts and seeds** (calorie-dense; easy to overeat)
- **Dried fruits** (calorie-concentrated; low satiety per calorie)
- **Alcohol** (7 kcal/g; displaces carbs/protein without satiety benefit)
- **Ultra-processed snacks** (chips, cookies, candy—engineered to override fullness)
- **Sugary drinks** (liquid calories don't trigger satiety; easy to overconsume)

**Occasional inclusion is fine** if it fits your calorie/macro targets and doesn't derail adherence.

## Summary

Staying full while cutting very lean comes down to:

1. **Protein at every meal** (highest satiety per calorie)
2. **High-volume, low-calorie vegetables** (fill your stomach without calories)
3. **Whole-grain and high-fiber carbs** (measured portions; maximize satiety)
4. **Minimal added fat** (maintain health floor; save calories for carbs)
5. **Consistency and patience** (adjust based on trend data, not daily noise)

The meals above provide **massive volume, high satiety, and adequate nutrition** while maintaining a calorie deficit. The key is choosing foods that let you eat large portions without exceeding your calorie target—and sticking with it long enough for the trend to show progress.
