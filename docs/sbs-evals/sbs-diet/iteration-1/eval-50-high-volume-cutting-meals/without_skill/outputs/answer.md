# High-Volume, Low-Calorie Meals for Cutting

## Core Principle

The key to staying full while cutting is maximizing **volume and satiety per calorie**. This means prioritizing:

- Lean proteins (high satiety, minimal fat)
- Vegetables (high volume, low calories)
- Whole grains in moderation (fiber = fullness)
- Minimal added fats and oils

---

## Practical Meal Combinations

### 1. **Protein + Vegetable Base**

- **Grilled chicken breast** (200g, ~330 cal) + **large mixed salad** (unlimited lettuce, tomatoes, cucumbers, peppers) + **balsamic vinegar** (minimal calories)
- **Lean ground turkey** (150g, ~240 cal) + **roasted broccoli and cauliflower** (400g, ~100 cal) + **brown rice** (100g cooked, ~110 cal)
- **White fish** (salmon or cod, 200g, ~200-280 cal) + **steamed asparagus** (300g, ~90 cal) + **sweet potato** (150g, ~130 cal)

### 2. **Egg-Based Meals**

- **Egg white omelet** (8-10 whites, ~120 cal) + **sautéed spinach and mushrooms** (200g, ~40 cal) + **whole wheat toast** (1 slice, ~80 cal)
- **Scrambled eggs** (2 whole eggs + 4 whites, ~200 cal) + **large portion of peppers and onions** (200g, ~50 cal)

### 3. **Lean Beef Options**

- **Extra-lean ground beef** (93/7, 150g, ~200 cal) + **large bowl of mixed vegetables** (broccoli, carrots, green beans, ~150g, ~60 cal) + **white rice** (100g cooked, ~130 cal)
- **Sirloin steak** (150g, ~240 cal) + **baked potato** (200g, ~160 cal) + **steamed broccoli** (300g, ~90 cal)

### 4. **Cottage Cheese Meals**

- **Low-fat cottage cheese** (200g, ~160 cal) + **berries** (150g, ~60 cal) + **granola** (20g, ~80 cal) = high protein, satisfying
- **Cottage cheese** (150g, ~120 cal) + **sliced tomatoes and cucumber** (200g, ~30 cal) + **whole grain crackers** (5-6, ~100 cal)

### 5. **Soup-Based Meals** (High Volume, Low Calorie)

- **Vegetable soup** (homemade with broth, lean meat, lots of vegetables) = 300-400g for 200-300 calories
- **Chicken and vegetable broth** (500ml, ~50 cal) + **shredded chicken** (100g, ~165 cal) + **noodles** (50g dry, ~180 cal) + **leafy greens** (100g, ~25 cal)
- **Lentil soup** (high fiber, high protein) = 300g serving ~250 calories

### 6. **Rice/Grain-Based Bowls**

- **White or brown rice** (150g cooked, ~195 cal) + **lean ground turkey** (120g, ~190 cal) + **large portion of stir-fried vegetables** (300g, ~80 cal) + **low-sodium soy sauce** (minimal calories)
- **Oatmeal** (50g dry, ~190 cal) + **egg whites** (4, ~60 cal) + **berries** (100g, ~40 cal)

### 7. **Pasta Meals** (Whole Grain)

- **Whole wheat pasta** (100g dry = 150g cooked, ~170 cal) + **lean ground beef** (100g, ~130 cal) + **marinara sauce** (100g, ~40 cal) + **steamed broccoli** (200g, ~60 cal)

### 8. **Snacks to Stay Full Between Meals**

- **Greek yogurt** (150g, ~100 cal) + **berries** (100g, ~40 cal)
- **Protein shake** (whey protein 25g, ~100 cal + water or unsweetened almond milk)
- **Popcorn** (air-popped, 30g, ~100 cal) = high volume, low calorie
- **Rice cakes** (2, ~70 cal) + **almond butter** (1 tbsp, ~95 cal)
- **Celery** with **peanut butter** (2 tbsp celery, ~20 cal + 1 tbsp PB, ~95 cal)

## Satiety Hacks

### Maximize Fullness Without Calories

1. **Drink water before meals** - fills stomach, reduces hunger
2. **Eat slowly** - takes 20 minutes for satiety signals to reach brain
3. **Prioritize protein** - most satiating macronutrient (25-30% of calories from protein)
4. **Add fiber** - vegetables, whole grains, legumes slow digestion
5. **Use spices and hot sauce** - capsaicin may slightly boost satiety
6. **Eat whole foods** - processed foods are less satiating per calorie
7. **Avoid liquid calories** - drinks don't trigger satiety like solid food

### Meal Timing

- **Eat protein at every meal** - maintains muscle, increases satiety
- **Spread meals throughout the day** - prevents extreme hunger
- **Eat larger meals earlier** - easier to manage hunger later in the day

---

## Calorie-Dense Foods to Minimize

While cutting, limit or avoid:

- **Oils and butter** (120 cal/tbsp) - use cooking spray or minimal amounts
- **Nuts and seeds** (high calorie density, easy to overeat)
- **Fried foods** - absorb oil, increasing calories
- **Sugary foods and drinks** - no satiety, quick hunger return
- **Alcohol** - 7 cal/gram, minimal satiety
- **Full-fat dairy** - choose low-fat or fat-free versions

## Key Takeaway

**High-volume, low-calorie eating = lean protein + vegetables + whole grains in moderation.** This combination maximizes satiety, preserves muscle during a cut, and makes the deficit sustainable. The goal is to eat a large volume of food that's nutrient-dense but calorie-sparse, so you feel full while maintaining a caloric deficit.
