# General-Purpose Exercise Selection: Core Movements for Health and Recreation

For someone focused on general health, recreation, and functional fitness (not competition), the exercise selection should prioritize compound movements that build strength, muscle, and work capacity across all major movement patterns.

## The Core Movement Patterns

All training should include these fundamental patterns:

1. **Horizontal pressing**: Bench press, dumbbell press, push-ups
2. **Vertical pressing**: Overhead press, pike push-ups
3. **Horizontal pulling**: Rows (barbell, dumbbell, machine)
4. **Vertical pulling**: Pull-ups, lat pulldowns, assisted pull-ups
5. **Lower body push**: Squat (back squat, front squat, goblet squat)
6. **Lower body pull**: Deadlift, trap bar deadlift, leg press
7. **Loaded carry**: Farmer's carry, suitcase carry, overhead carry

## Recommended Core Exercises

**Upper Body Pressing**:

- Barbell bench press or dumbbell press (primary)
- Overhead press (secondary)
- Incline press (optional, for shoulder health)

**Upper Body Pulling**:

- Barbell rows or dumbbell rows (primary)
- Pull-ups or lat pulldowns (secondary)
- Face pulls (for shoulder health)

**Lower Body**:

- Barbell back squat or goblet squat (primary)
- Deadlift or trap bar deadlift (primary)
- Leg press (optional, for volume)

**Loaded Carries**:

- Farmer's carry (primary)
- Suitcase carry (secondary)

**Accessory Work** (optional but recommended):

- Lateral raises (shoulder health)
- Leg curls (hamstring balance)
- Tricep extensions (arm health)
- Bicep curls (arm health)

## Why These Exercises?

1. **Compound movements**: They involve multiple joints and muscle groups, building functional strength.

2. **Efficient**: You get maximum benefit in minimum time. Compound movements are more time-efficient than isolation work.

3. **Scalable**: These exercises can be scaled for any fitness level (push-ups instead of bench press, assisted pull-ups, goblet squats).

4. **Functional**: They translate to real-world strength (picking things up, pushing, pulling, carrying).

5. **Safe**: When done with good form, these movements are relatively safe and low-injury risk.

## Sample Program Structure

**3-Day Full Body**:

- Day 1: Squat, bench press, rows, farmer's carry
- Day 2: Deadlift, overhead press, pull-ups, suitcase carry
- Day 3: Squat, rows, bench press, leg curls

**4-Day Upper/Lower**:

- Upper A: Bench press, rows, overhead press, face pulls
- Lower A: Squat, leg curls, farmer's carry
- Upper B: Overhead press, pull-ups, dumbbell rows, lateral raises
- Lower B: Deadlift, leg press, suitcase carry

**5-Day Push/Pull/Legs**:

- Push: Bench press, overhead press, lateral raises
- Pull: Rows, pull-ups, face pulls
- Legs: Squat, deadlift, leg curls, farmer's carry
- (Repeat 2 of the 3 days)

## Training Variables

**Volume**: 10–15 sets per muscle group per week is sufficient for general fitness.

**Intensity**: 6–12 reps per set is ideal. This builds strength and muscle without excessive joint stress.

**Frequency**: 3–4 days per week is sufficient for general fitness.

**Progression**: Add weight or reps every 1–2 weeks. This is the primary driver of progress.

## Important Principles

1. **Consistency beats perfection**: A simple program done consistently beats a perfect program done inconsistently.

2. **Progressive overload**: Gradually increase weight or reps. This is the primary driver of strength and muscle gain.

3. **Good form**: Proper form is more important than heavy weight. Bad form increases injury risk and reduces effectiveness.

4. **Recovery**: Sleep, nutrition, and stress management are as important as training.

5. **Sustainability**: Choose exercises you enjoy and can do long-term. A program you hate won't work.

## The Bottom Line

For general health and recreation, focus on compound movements across all major patterns: pressing, pulling, squatting, deadlifting, and carrying. These movements are efficient, functional, and safe. A simple program with 3–4 days per week, 10–15 sets per muscle group, and progressive overload is sufficient for excellent results.
