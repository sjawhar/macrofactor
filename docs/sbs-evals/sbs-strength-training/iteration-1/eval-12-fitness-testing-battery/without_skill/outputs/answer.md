# Fitness Testing Battery: Tracking Progress Every 12 Weeks

A comprehensive fitness testing battery should measure strength, power, endurance, and body composition. Testing every 12 weeks provides enough time to see meaningful progress while allowing frequent feedback.

## Recommended Testing Battery

**Strength Tests** (measure maximum force production):

1. **Back squat 1RM** (or 3RM if you prefer)
   - Measures lower body strength
   - Highly relevant for general fitness
   - Relatively safe and easy to test

2. **Deadlift 1RM** (or 3RM)
   - Measures posterior chain strength
   - Highly relevant for general fitness
   - Complements squat testing

3. **Bench press 1RM** (or 3RM)
   - Measures upper body pressing strength
   - Highly relevant for general fitness
   - Relatively safe and easy to test

4. **Overhead press 1RM** (or 3RM)
   - Measures shoulder pressing strength
   - Complements bench press
   - Good indicator of shoulder health

**Power Tests** (measure force production speed):

5. **Vertical jump**
   - Measures lower body power
   - Quick and easy to test
   - Highly relevant for athletic performance

6. **Broad jump**
   - Measures lower body power and coordination
   - Quick and easy to test
   - Complements vertical jump

**Endurance Tests** (measure work capacity):

7. **Max pull-ups** (or assisted pull-ups)
   - Measures upper body pulling strength and endurance
   - Quick and easy to test
   - Highly relevant for general fitness

8. **Max push-ups**
   - Measures upper body pressing endurance
   - Quick and easy to test
   - Complements pull-ups

9. **3-minute row** (or 500m row)
   - Measures aerobic capacity and work capacity
   - Requires rowing machine
   - Highly relevant for conditioning

**Body Composition**:

10. **Body weight**
    - Simple and objective
    - Tracks overall progress

11. **Body fat percentage** (optional)
    - Provides context for body weight changes
    - Can be measured via DEXA, BodPod, or skinfolds

## Testing Protocol

**Warm-up**:

- 5–10 minutes of light cardio
- Dynamic stretching
- Movement-specific warm-up sets

**Testing order**:

1. Strength tests first (when fresh)
2. Power tests second
3. Endurance tests last (when fatigued)
4. Body composition last

**Rest between tests**:

- 3–5 minutes between strength tests
- 2–3 minutes between power tests
- 1–2 minutes between endurance tests

**Consistency**:

- Test at the same time of day
- Same day of the week
- Same equipment and setup
- Same warm-up protocol

## Interpreting Results

**Strength gains**: 5–10% improvement per 12 weeks is excellent for intermediate lifters. Beginners might see 10–20% improvement.

**Power gains**: 2–5% improvement per 12 weeks is good. Power is harder to improve than strength.

**Endurance gains**: 5–10% improvement per 12 weeks is good. This depends heavily on training focus.

**Body composition**: 1–2 lbs per week of weight change is reasonable. If gaining muscle, expect 0.5–1 lb per week. If losing fat, expect 1–2 lbs per week.

## Adjusting Based on Results

**If strength is improving but endurance is declining**: You're probably training too much strength and not enough conditioning. Add more conditioning work.

**If endurance is improving but strength is declining**: You're probably training too much conditioning and not enough strength. Add more strength work.

**If body weight is increasing but body fat is increasing**: You're probably eating too much. Reduce calories slightly.

**If body weight is decreasing but strength is declining**: You're probably eating too little. Increase calories slightly.

## Simplified Version (Minimum Testing)

If full testing is too time-consuming, test these three:

1. **Squat or deadlift 1RM**: Measures lower body strength
2. **Bench press or overhead press 1RM**: Measures upper body strength
3. **Max pull-ups or push-ups**: Measures upper body endurance

This gives you a quick snapshot of overall progress.

## The Bottom Line

Test every 12 weeks using a battery that measures strength, power, endurance, and body composition. This provides objective feedback on progress and helps you adjust training. Consistency in testing protocol is more important than the specific tests chosen.
