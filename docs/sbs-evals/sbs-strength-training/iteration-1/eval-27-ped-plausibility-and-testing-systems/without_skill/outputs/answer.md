## PEDs in Professional Sports: Hidden Drugs vs. Overstated Claims

The truth is somewhere in the middle. There are likely some hidden or undetectable drugs, but the claim that "everyone is on PEDs" is probably overstated. The reality is more nuanced.

### What We Know About Testing

**Current testing detects:**

- Anabolic steroids (testosterone, nandrolone, trenbolone, etc.)
- Growth hormone (via biomarkers, not direct detection)
- EPO (erythropoietin)
- Stimulants (amphetamines, cocaine)
- Beta-blockers
- Diuretics

**Current testing doesn't detect well:**

- SARMs (selective androgen receptor modulators) — some tests exist but they're not universal
- Peptides (like BPC-157, TB-500) — very hard to detect
- Insulin — nearly impossible to detect
- Thyroid hormones — hard to distinguish from natural variation
- Novel compounds — anything new takes years to develop a test for

**Testing limitations:**

- Testing is expensive, so it's not done frequently in many sports
- Testing windows are known in advance in some sports, allowing athletes to time their use
- Some sports have weak testing infrastructure
- False negatives are common (tests miss positive cases)

### The Evidence for Hidden Drugs

**Plausible mechanisms:**

- Pharmaceutical companies develop new compounds constantly. Some are designed to be hard to detect.
- SARMs were developed specifically to avoid detection while providing anabolic effects
- Peptides are harder to detect than traditional steroids
- Insulin is nearly undetectable and has powerful anabolic effects

**Circumstantial evidence:**

- Some athletes test negative but show obvious signs of PED use (extreme muscularity, rapid changes)
- Some athletes have been caught using novel compounds that weren't on testing panels
- The gap between tested and untested athletes in some sports is enormous

**Likely hidden drugs:**

- SARMs (especially in sports with weak testing)
- Peptides (BPC-157, TB-500, others)
- Insulin (especially in bodybuilding)
- Novel compounds developed by pharmaceutical companies
- Combinations of drugs designed to evade detection

### The Evidence Against "Everyone is on PEDs"

**Testing does catch people:**

- Hundreds of athletes test positive each year
- Some high-profile athletes have been caught
- Testing has improved over time

**Not all elite performance requires PEDs:**

- Genetics matter enormously. Some people are naturally gifted
- Training and nutrition can produce remarkable results without drugs
- The difference between elite and non-elite is often 5-10%, not 100%

**Variability in testing:**

- Some sports have rigorous testing (Olympic weightlifting, track and field)
- Others have weak testing (bodybuilding, some strength sports)
- The prevalence of PED use varies by sport

**Survivor bias:**

- We see the athletes who succeed. We don't see the many who fail despite using PEDs
- Some athletes use PEDs and still don't reach elite levels
- Natural athletes do reach elite levels, though they're probably a minority

### Sport-Specific Reality

**Olympic sports (track, weightlifting, swimming):**

- Testing is relatively rigorous
- PED use is common but not universal
- Estimate: 20-40% of elite athletes use PEDs
- Many elite athletes are likely natural, though they're a minority

**Bodybuilding:**

- Testing is weak or absent
- PED use is nearly universal at the elite level
- Estimate: 80-95% of elite bodybuilders use PEDs
- Natural bodybuilders exist but are rare at the highest levels

**Powerlifting:**

- Testing varies by federation
- IPF (International Powerlifting Federation) has rigorous testing
- Other federations have weak testing
- Estimate: 30-50% in tested federations, 70-90% in untested federations

**American football:**

- Testing exists but is limited
- PED use is common but not universal
- Estimate: 30-50% of elite players use PEDs

**Baseball:**

- Testing has improved over time
- PED use was rampant in the 1990s-2000s, less so now
- Estimate: 10-30% of current elite players use PEDs

### Why the Claim "Everyone is on PEDs" is Overstated

1. **Testing does work.** If everyone used PEDs, we'd expect more positive tests. The fact that testing catches people suggests it's not universal.

2. **Genetics matter.** Some people are naturally gifted. A 6'5" person with naturally high testosterone and fast-twitch muscle fibers can reach elite levels without drugs.

3. **Training and nutrition matter.** Decades of consistent training and optimal nutrition can produce elite-level performance without drugs.

4. **Variability by sport.** Some sports have much higher PED use than others. Saying "everyone" is on PEDs ignores this variation.

5. **Survivor bias.** We see the successful athletes. We don't see the many who use PEDs and fail.

### Why the Claim "PEDs are Rare" is Also Wrong

1. **Testing is imperfect.** Many drugs are hard to detect. The absence of a positive test doesn't mean the athlete is natural.

2. **Hidden drugs exist.** SARMs, peptides, and novel compounds are hard to detect and likely used by some athletes.

3. **Testing is inconsistent.** Some sports have weak testing. Athletes in those sports use PEDs at high rates.

4. **The performance gap is large.** In some sports, the gap between elite and non-elite is so large that it's hard to explain without PEDs.

### The Honest Answer

In most professional sports, PED use is common but not universal. The prevalence varies by sport, testing rigor, and individual athlete choice. Some athletes reach elite levels naturally (though they're probably a minority). Others use PEDs. Some use hidden or undetectable drugs.

The claim that "everyone is on PEDs" is overstated because:

- Testing does catch people
- Genetics and training matter
- Not all sports have equally high PED use

The claim that "PEDs are rare" is also wrong because:

- Testing is imperfect
- Hidden drugs likely exist
- The performance gap in some sports is hard to explain without drugs

The realistic estimate is that 20-50% of elite athletes in tested sports use PEDs, and 70-90% in untested sports. This varies by sport, federation, and era.

### Bottom Line

There are likely some hidden or undetectable drugs used by elite athletes. But the claim that "everyone is on PEDs" is overstated. The reality is that PED use is common in professional sports, especially in untested sports, but it's not universal. Genetics, training, and nutrition still matter. Testing does work, though it's imperfect. The prevalence of PED use varies significantly by sport and testing rigor.
