# Evidence-Based Supplements: What Works vs Hype

The supplement industry is full of marketing claims. Here's what the evidence actually supports, ranked by strength of evidence.

## Tier 1: Strong Evidence (Worth Taking)

**Creatine Monohydrate**

- Strongest evidence of any supplement
- 3-5g daily increases strength and muscle mass by ~5-10%
- Improves power output and training capacity
- Safe, cheap, well-researched
- Effect size: Large

**Protein Powder**

- Not technically a supplement, but essential for meeting protein targets
- Convenient way to reach 0.7-1g per lb bodyweight
- Whey, casein, and plant-based all work
- Effect size: Large (if you're not meeting protein targets)

**Caffeine**

- 3-6mg per kg bodyweight improves strength, power, and endurance
- Reduces perceived effort
- Well-researched, safe
- Effect size: Moderate

## Tier 2: Moderate Evidence (Probably Worth Taking)

**Beta-Alanine**

- Increases muscle carnosine, buffering fatigue
- Improves performance in 1-4 minute efforts (6-10 reps)
- Requires 3-5g daily for 4+ weeks to see effects
- Effect size: Small to moderate

**Citrulline Malate**

- Improves blood flow and reduces fatigue
- 6-8g pre-workout may improve pump and endurance
- Effect size: Small to moderate

**Sodium Bicarbonate**

- Buffers lactate, improving high-rep performance
- 0.3g per kg bodyweight 60 minutes pre-workout
- Causes GI distress in many people
- Effect size: Small

## Tier 3: Weak Evidence (Probably Not Worth It)

**BCAAs (Branched-Chain Amino Acids)**

- Whole protein is superior
- Only useful if you're fasted and can't eat protein
- Effect size: Negligible if protein intake is adequate

**Testosterone Boosters**

- Most have minimal evidence
- Tribulus, fenugreek, D-aspartate show weak effects at best
- Effect size: Negligible to small

**Fat Burners**

- Caffeine is the only ingredient with real evidence
- Most other ingredients lack human studies
- Effect size: Negligible

**Glutamine**

- Minimal evidence for strength or hypertrophy
- Your body produces enough
- Effect size: Negligible

## Tier 4: No Evidence / Avoid

**Proprietary Blends**

- Undisclosed ingredient amounts
- Impossible to assess effectiveness
- Often contain ineffective ingredients at low doses

**Expensive "Cutting Edge" Supplements**

- New doesn't mean better
- Most lack human research
- Marketing > science

## Practical Supplement Stack

If you want to supplement evidence-based:

1. **Protein powder** - If you can't meet protein targets with food
2. **Creatine monohydrate** - 5g daily, cheap and effective
3. **Caffeine** - Pre-workout if you tolerate it
4. **Multivitamin** - If your diet is poor (optional)

Total cost: ~$30-50/month

Everything else is optional or ineffective. The biggest ROI comes from:

- Adequate protein intake
- Consistent training
- Progressive overload
- Sleep and recovery
- Stress management

Supplements are 5% of the equation. Get the fundamentals right first.
