# Yes, You Can Progress on 1 Squat Day/Week

Short answer: absolutely yes. Dropping from 2-3 squat sessions per week to 1 does **not** mean progress stops; it usually means progress may be **slower on average** because you get less practice and less distributed volume.

From an SBS lens, frequency is mostly a tool for three things: skill exposure, volume distribution, and fatigue management. Intermediates often do well at 2-3 exposures/week, but 1 exposure can still be productive if the session is set up intelligently.

## The Main Setup Rule: Don’t "Compress" Everything Into One Marathon Session

If you were doing ~15 hard squat sets/week across multiple days, don’t immediately force all 15 into one day. Quality will likely collapse, bar speed will drop, and late sets will be low-value fatigue.

SBS-style practical caps suggest keeping hard sets per large muscle group in a manageable range per session (often around **4-8 high-quality sets** before returns diminish). You can push above that occasionally, but not as a default if performance is the goal.

## A Better Starting Heuristic

Use your old per-session average as the anchor, then add a little.

- If you were at 15 sets/week over 3 days (about 5/session), start your one weekly day around **6-7 hard squat sets**.
- If you were at 12 sets/week over 2 days (about 6/session), start around **7-8 sets**.

That follows the "previous session dose + 1-2 sets" rule of thumb and avoids a drastic underdose while keeping quality high.

Also, try not to cut weekly squat volume by more than about **50%** at first. So if you came from 14-16 sets/week, beginning near 7-9 sets in one session is usually a reasonable trial point.

## How to Progress It Week to Week

Use a simple decision ladder for 3-4 weeks:

1. **Performance improving at matched effort (1-3 RIR on compounds)?** Keep volume steady.
2. **Performance flat but recovery good?** Add 1 set (or a small load bump).
3. **Performance down + recovery poor?** Don’t add volume; consider trimming 1-2 sets or using a light week.

Keep most main squat work in the usual SBS strength-hypertrophy zone: roughly **3-8 reps**, mostly **70-85%**, with occasional heavier exposures.

## Practical One-Day Template

- Competition squat: 4-5 sets of 3-6 reps @ ~72-82% (1-3 RIR)
- Secondary squat variant (pause or tempo): 2-3 sets of 4-6 reps @ ~65-75%
- Optional quad accessory (if needed): 1-3 sets of 6-12 reps

Bottom line: once-weekly squatting works. Just make that day **bigger than your old average session**, not absurdly huge, then titrate based on actual performance and recovery.
