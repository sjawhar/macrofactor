# Reducing Squat Frequency: Can You Progress on 1 Day Per Week?

Yes, you can progress on one squat day per week, but you'll likely progress slower than with 2–3 days. Here's what the research and practical experience show:

## The Frequency Question

**What research shows**: Training frequency (how often you train a muscle per week) affects hypertrophy and strength, but the relationship is nuanced:

- 1x per week: Produces hypertrophy and strength gains, but slower than higher frequencies
- 2–3x per week: Optimal for most people, balancing stimulus and recovery
- 4+ x per week: Diminishing returns for most, though some advanced lifters benefit

**For strength specifically**: Frequency matters less than total volume. You can gain strength on 1x per week if volume is adequate.

## Practical Considerations

**Advantages of 1x per week**:

- Simpler programming
- Lower recovery demands
- Easier to manage if you have limited time
- Allows higher volume per session without excessive fatigue

**Disadvantages of 1x per week**:

- Slower progress (roughly 30–50% slower than 2–3x per week)
- Longer recovery between sessions (7 days) means more detraining between workouts
- Less frequent stimulus for hypertrophy
- Harder to accumulate adequate volume in one session without excessive fatigue

## How to Make It Work

If you're reducing from 2–3 to 1 squat day per week, follow these principles:

**Maintain or increase volume**: Do 12–16 sets of squat-focused work per week (all in one session). This might include:

- 4–5 sets of heavy squats (3–5 reps)
- 6–8 sets of moderate squats (6–10 reps)
- 3–4 sets of squat variations (leg press, hack squat, etc.)

**Prioritize intensity**: With only one session, ensure you're training close to failure on at least some sets. This maximizes stimulus per session.

**Manage fatigue**: One high-volume squat session is taxing. Ensure adequate recovery (sleep, nutrition) and don't add excessive volume elsewhere.

**Track progress carefully**: Progress will be slower. Expect strength gains of 2.5–5 lbs per month rather than 5–10 lbs.

## Realistic Expectations

You can definitely progress on 1x per week, but:

- Progress will be 30–50% slower than 2–3x per week
- You'll need to be more disciplined about volume and intensity
- Recovery becomes more critical
- Long-term, you may plateau sooner than with higher frequency

## Bottom Line

Yes, you can progress on 1 squat day per week. Maintain high volume (12–16 sets), prioritize intensity, and be patient with progress. If you were making good progress at 2–3x per week, expect slower gains at 1x per week. If your schedule demands it, it's viable—just manage expectations and ensure adequate volume and recovery.
