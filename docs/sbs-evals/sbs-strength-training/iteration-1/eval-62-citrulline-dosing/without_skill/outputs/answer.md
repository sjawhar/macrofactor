# L-Citrulline: Dosing, Timing, and Efficacy

L-citrulline is a non-essential amino acid that boosts nitric oxide (NO) production, improving blood flow, endurance, and potentially strength and hypertrophy. Here's what the evidence shows about whether you should use it, how much, and when.

## Should You Take It?

L-citrulline has solid evidence for:

- **Endurance performance**: 4-6% improvements in time-to-exhaustion and time trials
- **Strength and power**: Modest gains in repetitions to failure and power output
- **Muscle soreness**: Reduced DOMS (delayed-onset muscle soreness) by 20-40%
- **Pump and vascularity**: Improved blood flow and nutrient delivery

The effect size is moderate but consistent across studies. It's not a game-changer, but it's one of the few supplements with genuine evidence behind it.

## Dosing

**Effective dose**: 6-8 grams per day for performance benefits

- Some studies use up to 10g, but 6-8g is the sweet spot for most people
- Doses below 5g show minimal effects
- More than 10g doesn't provide additional benefit and may cause GI distress

**Form matters**:

- **L-citrulline malate** (6-8g): Most common form; the malate may provide additional fatigue-buffering benefits
- **Citrulline malate 2:1 ratio** (8g citrulline malate = ~4g citrulline): Adjust dosing accordingly
- **Pure L-citrulline** (6-8g): Works equally well but lacks the malate component

## Timing

**Pre-workout is optimal**: Take 6-8g of L-citrulline 60-90 minutes before training

- Peak plasma levels occur around 60 minutes post-ingestion
- This timing maximizes blood flow during your workout
- Taking it with a small amount of carbs or protein may improve absorption

**Consistency matters more than timing**: Daily intake is more important than perfect timing. If you take it post-workout or with meals, you'll still see benefits—just not as acutely during that session.

## Practical Considerations

**Stacking**: L-citrulline works well with:

- Beetroot juice or dietary nitrate (synergistic NO production)
- Beta-alanine (different mechanism, complementary effects)
- Caffeine (independent mechanisms)

**Cost**: L-citrulline is inexpensive (~$0.10-0.20 per dose), making it one of the best value supplements.

**Side effects**: Generally well-tolerated. High doses (>10g) may cause mild GI distress or diarrhea in sensitive individuals.

**Individual variability**: Some people respond strongly; others see minimal effects. This is normal with most supplements.

## Bottom Line

If you're serious about strength training or endurance, L-citrulline is worth trying. Take 6-8g of L-citrulline malate 60-90 minutes before training. It's cheap, safe, and has legitimate evidence. If you don't notice improvements after 2-3 weeks of consistent use, it may not be for you—individual response varies. Don't expect dramatic changes, but expect modest improvements in endurance, strength, and recovery.
